Perform a steady-state simulation of turbulent, compressible water flow through a square bend using the rhoSimpleFoam solver with the k-epsilon turbulence model. The working fluid is H₂O modeled as a liquid with sensible internal energy. The inlet imposes a mass flow rate of 5 kg/s with 5% turbulent intensity and a 5mm mixing length for dissipation rate initialization. Outlet pressure is fixed at 1e5 Pa. All walls are no-slip boundaries with constant temperature of 350 K and wall functions for turbulence quantities.

Discretization uses steady-state time derivatives, limited Gauss linear gradients, and bounded linearUpwind schemes for divergence terms, with corrected Gauss linear laplacian schemes. The SIMPLE algorithm solves pressure with GAMG and velocity/turbulence variables with smoothSolver, both using relative tolerances of 0.1. Apply under-relaxation factors of 0.3 for pressure and 0.7 for velocity and turbulence equations. Residual convergence thresholds are 1e-4 for pressure/velocity and 1e-3 for turbulence quantities.
