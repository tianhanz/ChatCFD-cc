We simulate a laminar solitary wave generated by a wave maker using the interFoam solver, solving incompressible, multiphase flow of water and air. The case utilizes a dynamic mesh with the displacementLaplacian solver, coupled to wave generation at the inlet boundary. Key fluid properties include water density (1000 kg/m³), air density (1 kg/m³), water kinematic viscosity (1e-06 m²/s), air kinematic viscosity (1.48e-05 m²/s), and surface tension coefficient (0.07 N/m). Gravity acts downward at -9.81 m/s².

Spatial discretization employs Gauss-linear schemes for gradients and laplacians, vanLeer for divergence of alpha, and linearUpwind for momentum. Time integration uses Euler scheme. Pressure-velocity coupling follows the PIMPLE algorithm with 2 pressure correctors and no non-orthogonal correctors. Solvers include PCG-DIC for pressure, GAMG with GaussSeidel smoother for mesh displacement, and PBiCG-DILU for velocity. The volume fraction field uses smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel and MULES compression. Initial conditions set uniform zero velocity, zero pressure, and zero water fraction throughout the domain. The wave maker inlet applies solitary wave motion with height 0.1 m, water depth 0.2 m, period 1.0 s, and ramp-up time 1.0 s. All solid boundaries use no-slip velocity conditions and fixed-flux pressure.
