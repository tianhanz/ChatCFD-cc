Employ the rhoCentralFoam solver to conduct a 1-dimensional laminar simulation of a compressible shock tube flow using the ideal gas model. The fluid properties are defined with constant specific heat (Cp = 1004.5 J/kg·K) and zero viscosity. Initial conditions set pressure and velocity uniformly to zero, while temperature is initialized uniformly at 1 K. 

Divergence terms use the Kurganov flux scheme with vanLeer reconstruction for density, velocity, and temperature. Gradient terms apply Gauss linear discretization, and the Euler scheme handles time derivatives. The simulation employs an explicit diagonal solver for continuity and energy equations. For momentum and enthalpy equations, a smoothSolver with Gauss-Seidel smoother performs two sweeps per iteration, using absolute tolerance 1e-9 for velocity and 1e-10 for enthalpy with relative tolerance settings of 0.01 and 0 respectively. Boundary conditions apply zeroGradient for sides and empty for ends.
