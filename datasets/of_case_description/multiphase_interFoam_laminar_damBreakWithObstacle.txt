This simulation uses the interFoam solver to model a 2D dam-break flow with an obstacle under laminar conditions. Two Newtonian fluids are present: water (density 1000 kg/m³, viscosity 1e-06 m²/s) and air (density 1 kg/m³, viscosity 1.48e-05 m²/s), with a surface tension coefficient of 0.07 N/m. Gravity acts downward with -9.81 m/s² in the z-direction. The initial conditions set the entire domain with zero water volume fraction, zero velocity, and zero modified pressure (p_rgh).

Dynamic mesh refinement activates based on the water volume fraction field (alpha.water), refining cells where the value is between 0.001 and 0.999, with a maximum refinement level of 2. The solution scheme employs a PIMPLE algorithm with 3 corrector iterations and no momentum prediction. Pressure is solved using the GAMG method with DIC smoothing and 1e-8 tolerance, while velocity uses a smoothSolver with Gauss-Seidel smoothing and 1e-6 tolerance. The alpha.water equation applies the vanLeer divergence scheme for interface sharpness. Boundary conditions enforce no-slip walls with fixed zero velocity, atmospheric pressure at the top boundary, and zero-gradient conditions for phase fraction at solid surfaces.
