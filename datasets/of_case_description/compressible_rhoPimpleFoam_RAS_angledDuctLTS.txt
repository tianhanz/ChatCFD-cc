This simulation employs rhoPimpleFoam to model compressible, turbulent flow within an angled duct. Key physical models include the k-epsilon turbulence closure defined in constant/turbulenceProperties, and Sutherland's law transport with perfect gas equation of state specified in constant/thermophysicalProperties. An explicit Darcy-Forchheimer porosity model is applied within a specific cellZone via constant/fvOptions.

The discretization in system/fvSchemes uses localEuler time derivatives (ddtSchemes). Gradient terms default to Gauss linear. Convection terms primarily use an upwind scheme: div(phi,U) and div(phi,h) use Gauss upwind. Turbulent kinetic energy (k) and dissipation rate (epsilon) convection also use Gauss upwind via the '$turbulence' specification. Diffusion terms employ Gauss linear corrected.

Solution settings (system/fvSolution) utilize the PIMPLE algorithm with momentum prediction enabled (momentumPredictor yes), performing 1 outer corrector and 1 pressure corrector per time step (nOuterCorrectors 1, nCorrectors 1). The pressure (p) is solved using the PCG method with DIC preconditioning. Velocity (U), enthalpy (h), and turbulence fields (k, epsilon) are solved using a smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel smoother.

Initial conditions include a uniform velocity of (47.8, 0, 0) m/s, pressure of 1.0e5 Pa, temperature of 293 K, turbulence kinetic energy (k) of 1 m²/s², and dissipation rate (epsilon) of 200 m²/s³. Boundary conditions define solid walls ('front', 'back', 'walls') with no-slip velocity and specialized k-epsilon wall functions (nutkWallFunction, kqRWallFunction, epsilonWallFunction, alphatWallFunction). A slip condition is applied at the 'porosityWall'. The inlet uses a fixed mass flow rate (0.1 kg/s) specification via flowRateInletVelocity for velocity and turbulentIntensityKineticEnergyInlet for k. The outlet uses fixedValue pressure and inletOutlet conditions for other quantities.
