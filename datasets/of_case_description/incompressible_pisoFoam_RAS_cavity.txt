Simulating a lid-driven cavity flow in OpenFOAM using the pisoFoam solver with a k-epsilon turbulence model. The case involves an incompressible, turbulent flow of a Newtonian fluid with kinematic viscosity ν = 1e-05 m²/s. The cavity has three stationary walls and one moving top wall sliding tangentially at 1 m/s in the x-direction. Initial velocity is zero throughout the domain except at the moving wall. Pressure, turbulence kinetic energy (k), and dissipation rate (epsilon) fields are initialized uniformly at 0, 0.00375 m²/s², and 0.00754 m²/s³ respectively.

Discretization schemes employ Gauss linear gradients and corrected snGrad schemes. Convection terms for momentum use a CoBlended scheme blending limitedLinearV and linearUpwind based on Courant number. Turbulence convection applies limitedLinear divergence. Pressure is solved via GAMG with Gauss-Seidel smoothing, while velocity and turbulence fields use smoothSolver with Gauss-Seidel. PISO algorithm performs 2 pressure correctors with no non-orthogonal correction. Boundary conditions use wall functions for turbulence quantities (kqRWallFunction for k, epsilonWallFunction for ε), fixedValue/noSlip for velocity, and zeroGradient for pressure at walls.
