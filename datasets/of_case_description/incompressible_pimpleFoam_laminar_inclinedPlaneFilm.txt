Using pimpleFoam to simulate a laminar thin liquid film flow over an inclined plane under gravity. The fluid is Newtonian with kinematic viscosity nu = 1e-5 m²/s. Gravity acts with components (4.905, 0, -8.4957) m/s². The simulation employs a specialized velocity-film boundary condition at the film surface, handling interfacial dynamics. Initial film thickness is 10 μm uniformly, with inlet film thickness set to 0.141 mm. Fluid enters at the inlet with uniform velocity (1, 0, 0) m/s, while cylinder boundaries are fixed at no-slip conditions.

Discretization uses Euler scheme for time derivatives. Gradient terms apply Gauss linear method. Divergence of momentum flux uses Gauss linearUpwind with grad(U) interpolation. Laplacian terms use Gauss linear corrected with snGrad corrected. Pressure solver employs GAMG with Gauss-Seidel smoother and tolerance 1e-6. Velocity solver uses smoothSolver with Gauss-Seidel smoother and tolerance 1e-5. PIMPLE algorithm performs 1 outer correction and 1 pressure-velocity corrector without non-orthogonal correction. No relaxation is applied to velocity equations. Initial relative pressure and velocity fields are zero throughout the domain.
