This is a steady-state RANS simulation using the `simpleFoam` solver for incompressible turbulent flow. The `kOmegaSSTLM` turbulence model is employed, specifically incorporating the local correlation-based transition modeling capability. The fluid is Newtonian with a kinematic viscosity of 1.5e-5 m²/s. The simulation setup targets an external aerodynamic flow over a plate, indicated by boundary types.

The pressure field `p` uses a fixed value zero-gradient condition at the inlet and fixed value zero at the outlet. The inlet velocity `U` is uniformly set to 5.4 m/s in the x-direction. The plate boundary has a no-slip condition for velocity, while the top and above boundaries use slip conditions. Initial and boundary conditions for turbulence fields `k` (turbulent kinetic energy) and `omega` (specific dissipation rate), as well as the transition model fields `gammaInt` (intermittency) and `ReThetat` (transition momentum thickness Reynolds number), are uniformly specified at the inlet.

For spatial discretization:
*   Gradients use `Gauss linear`.
*   Divergence terms employ a `bounded Gauss linearUpwind grad` scheme for momentum (`phi,U`) and the turbulence/transition fields (`phi,k`, `phi,omega`, `phi,gammaInt`, `phi,ReThetat`), providing upwind-biased stability.
*   Laplacians use `Gauss linear corrected`.
*   Surface normal gradients use `corrected`.
*   Wall distance calculation uses the `meshWave` method.

The `fvSolution` settings configure:
*   The pressure (`p`) system is solved with the Geometric-Algebraic Multi-Grid (GAMG) solver, tolerances 1e-6 absolute and 0.1 relative, using Gauss-Seidel smoothing.
*   Velocity, turbulence, and transition fields (`U, k, omega, gammaInt, ReThetat`) use the `smoothSolver` with symmetric Gauss-Seidel smoother, tolerances 1e-8 absolute and 0.1 relative, limiting iterations to 10 per SIMPLE loop.
*   Residual controls enforce stopping criteria of 1e-5 for pressure, 1e-6 for velocity, and 1e-4 for turbulence and transition fields.
*   Under-relaxation of 0.9 is applied to all equations.
