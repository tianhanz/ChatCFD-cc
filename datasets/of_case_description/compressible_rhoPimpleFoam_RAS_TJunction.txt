The simulation involves compressible turbulent flow in a T-junction geometry using the rhoPimpleFoam solver with the k-epsilon turbulence model. The fluid is air treated as a perfect gas with constant specific heat (Cp=1007 J/kgK) and Sutherland's law for viscosity. Viscous dissipation effects are included via an fvOptions source term. Initial field values are uniform: pressure at 1e5 Pa, temperature at 300 K, velocity zero, turbulent kinetic energy at 1 m²/s², and dissipation rate at 200 m²/s³.

Boundary conditions are configured as follows: inlet uses uniform total pressure (ramping from 1e5 to 1.4e5 Pa) with pressureInletOutletVelocity; both outlets are fixed-value pressure at 1e5 Pa with inletOutlet velocity; walls impose no-slip velocity, kqRWallFunction for turbulence, epsilonWallFunction for dissipation, and zero-gradient temperature. A specialized heat addition of 5 W is applied at the inlet via outletMappedUniformInletHeatAddition, referencing outlet1 for mapping. The discretization employs an Euler scheme for time derivatives, Gauss linear for gradients, and limitedLinear for divergence schemes (limitedLinearV for momentum). Pressure equations use GAMG linear solvers with tight tolerances (1e-8), while turbulence variables use smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel. The PIMPLE algorithm runs 5 pressure-velocity correctors per outer iteration without momentum prediction. Relaxation factors are set to 1 for all equations.
