Utilizing the rhoPimpleFoam solver, this simulation models compressible turbulent flow over a NACA0012 airfoil at room temperature conditions. The turbulence closure employs the k-Omega SST model. The working fluid is air, treated as a perfect gas with constant specific heat (Cp=1005 J/kg·K) and viscosity (mu=1.82e-05 Pa·s). Freestream conditions include a velocity of 200 m/s, pressure of 1e5 Pa, temperature of 298 K, turbulent kinetic energy of 0.01 m²/s², and specific dissipation rate of 10 rad/s. 

Discretization applies Euler time integration, Gauss linear gradients with cell limiting for U/k/omega, linear-upwind convection for momentum/turbulence/energy terms, and upwind divergence for pressure-velocity coupling. Laplacian terms use corrected Gaussian schemes.

Solution settings feature GAMG with Gauss-Seidel smoother for pressure (tolerance 1e-6, relTol 0.01), and PBiCGStab with DILU preconditioner for momentum/turbulence equations (tolerance 1e-6, relTol 0.1). The PIMPLE algorithm uses 2 correctors and 1 non-orthogonal correction. Residual targets are 1e-4 for pressure/velocity/turbulence variables. Temperature is constrained between 101 K and 1000 K via limiters. Wall functions enforce near-wall turbulence treatment and no-slip velocity conditions.
