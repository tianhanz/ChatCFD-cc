Using the interFoam solver, conduct a 3D laminar simulation of sloshing liquid (water) in a moving tank with three degrees of freedom. The tank undergoes prescribed rigid body motion defined by the SDA model with parameters: CofG (0 0 0), lamda 50, rollAmax 0.2, rollAmin 0.1, heaveA 4, swayA 2.4, Q 2, Tp 14, Tpn 12, dTi 0.06, dTp -0.001. The multiphase system involves Newtonian water (density 998.2 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1e-06 m²/s) and Newtonian air (density 1 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1.48e-05 m²/s), with surface tension coefficient sigma set to 0. Initially, the tank is empty (volume fraction of water alpha.water = 0 everywhere), fluid velocity U is stationary (0 0 0), and modified pressure p_rgh is zero. Time discretization uses the Euler scheme. Gradient terms use Gauss linear interpolation. Divergence schemes are vanLeerV for rhoPhi,U and vanLeer for alpha terms. The PIMPLE algorithm is employed with momentum prediction disabled, 2 pressure-velocity correctors, no non-orthogonal correctors, and phi correction disabled. Solver settings include: GAMG with DIC smoother for p_rgh (relTol 0.01), smoothSolver with GaussSeidel for U, and specialized alpha sub-cycling with 3 sub-cycles and cAlpha 1.5. Reference pressure is 1e5 Pa at point (0 0 0.15). No relaxation factors are applied to velocity equations. Gravity is (0 0 -9.81) m/s².
