This simulation involves a 2D rotating mixer vessel using the rhoPimpleFoam solver for compressible transient flows with the k-epsilon turbulence model. The geometry includes rotor and stator zones. The fluid is air modeled as a perfect gas with constant properties. Initial conditions feature uniform static pressure, temperature, and turbulence fields at atmospheric conditions with zero velocity. Boundary conditions apply no-slip walls for velocity, wall functions for turbulence quantities, and pressure gradient constraints. 

The solver employs a PIMPLE algorithm with pressure predictor and two corrector steps, using GAMG with Gauss-Seidel smoothing for pressure and smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel for velocity and turbulence equations. Discretization schemes use limited linear divergence for momentum and scalar transport, linear corrected Gauss laplacian, and corrected surface normal gradients. Turbulent viscosity is resolved via k-epsilon closure. A Darcy-Forchheimer porous zone is applied to the stator region to model flow resistance.
