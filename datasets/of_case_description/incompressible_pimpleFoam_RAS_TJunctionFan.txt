This OpenFOAM simulation models incompressible, turbulent fluid flow through a T-junction with a fan-driven inlet using the transient solver pimpleFoam. The RANS turbulence closure employs the standard k-epsilon model. The fluid is Newtonian with kinematic viscosity set to 1e-05 m²/s.

Discretization uses a limitedLinear scheme for velocity divergence and turbulence transport terms (k, epsilon), Gauss linear for gradients, and Euler time integration. Pressure solving applies the GAMG algorithm with a tolerance of 1e-06 and 0.01 relative tolerance. Momentum and turbulence equations are solved using a smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel smoothing, tolerances of 1e-05, and 0.1 relative tolerance. The PIMPLE algorithm executes with 1 outer corrector and 2 pressure-velocity correctors per timestep, with no relaxation applied.

Boundary conditions specify a fan pressure inlet (pressure reference: 30 Pa), fixed-value pressure outlets (10 Pa and 0 Pa), and no-slip walls. Turbulent kinetic energy at the inlet sets a 5% intensity, while dissipation uses a mixing length of 0.01 m. Wall functions enforce turbulence quantities at boundaries. The simulation domain initializes with zero velocity and turbulent viscosity, uniform pressure (0 Pa), turbulent kinetic energy (0.375 m²/s²), and dissipation rate (200 m²/s³).
