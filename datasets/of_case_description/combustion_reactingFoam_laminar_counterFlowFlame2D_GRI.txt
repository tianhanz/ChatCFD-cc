Conduct a 2D laminar counter-flow flame simulation using reactingFoam with the GRI chemical mechanism. The setup involves opposing fuel (pure CH4) and air (23% O2, 77% N2) streams flowing at 0.1 m/s each under atmospheric pressure (1e5 Pa), with initial temperatures at 293 K inlets and a hot internal field of 2000 K. Species transport includes CH4, O2, N2, H2O, CO2, and a default inert field. 

Discretization employs Euler time scheme, Gauss linear gradients, limited linear divergence for momentum/species/energy, and orthogonal surface normal gradients. Pressure is solved via PCG/DIC with 1e-6 tolerance; velocity/enthalpy use PBiCGStab/DILU with 1e-6 tolerance. PIMPLE coupling disables momentum prediction and uses 1 outer correction with 2 inner corrections. Chemistry applies the ODE solver (seulex) with adaptive time stepping and specified species thermodynamic properties. Boundary conditions include fixed species fractions at inlets, zero-gradient pressure at inlets, total pressure at outlet, and pressure-driven velocity conditions.
