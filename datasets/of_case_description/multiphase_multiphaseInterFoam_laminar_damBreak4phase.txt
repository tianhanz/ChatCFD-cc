Employ the multiphaseInterFoam solver to conduct a four-phase laminar dam break simulation involving water, oil, mercury, and air. The setup includes gravity acting vertically downward at 9.81 m/s². Water has a density of 1000 kg/m³ and viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s, oil 500 kg/m³ and 1e-06 m²/s, mercury 13529 kg/m³ and 1.125e-07 m²/s, and air 1 kg/m³ and 1.48e-05 m²/s. Uniform surface tension of 0.07 N/m applies between all fluid pairs. Initial conditions set velocity to zero everywhere, modified pressure p_rgh to zero, and volume fractions of all non-air phases to zero internally with atmospheric boundaries favoring air ingress. Walls enforce no-slip velocity conditions and 90° contact angles for interfacial interactions. Discretization uses Euler time schemes, Gauss linear gradients, vanLeer convection for phase fraction transport, and upwind for momentum. Solvers deploy GAMG/PCG for pressure and smoothSolver/GaussSeidel for velocity, with PIMPLE handling 2 pressure-velocity correctors. No turbulence modeling is activated.
