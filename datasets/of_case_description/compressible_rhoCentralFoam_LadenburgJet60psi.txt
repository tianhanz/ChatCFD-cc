This simulation employs the rhoCentralFoam solver to model a laminar, compressible gas flow for the LadenburgJet60psi case, representing a supersonic jet configuration. The fluid is air treated as a perfect gas with Sutherland viscosity transport and constant specific heat capacity. The 2D axisymmetric domain uses wedge boundaries to reduce dimensionality. Initial non-uniform fields are defined for pressure, velocity (predominantly axial), and temperature. Boundary conditions include an inlet with fixed pressure (271724 Pa) and velocity (315.6 m/s axial component), an outlet with wave-transmissive pressure condition, and freestream boundaries with no-slip velocity and fixed temperature (297 K). The numerical setup utilizes the Kurganov flux scheme with vanLeer reconstruction for density, velocity and temperature. Euler time discretization and Gauss linear gradient schemes are applied. Solution algorithms employ a diagonal solver for density/energy fields and a smooth Gauss-Seidel solver for velocity with tight tolerances (1e-10). Turbulence modeling is explicitly disabled via the laminar simulation setting.
