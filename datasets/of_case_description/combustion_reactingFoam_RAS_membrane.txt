The simulation employs the reactingFoam solver to model reactive gas flow involving mass transfer across a semi-permeable membrane using the k-epsilon RAS turbulence model. The setup features a mixture of CH4, O2, H2O, CO2, and N2, with an Arrhenius-based methane combustion reaction defined in the chemistry model, though chemistry solving is explicitly disabled. The thermophysical properties follow the perfect gas equation of state with sutherland transport and JANAF thermodynamics.

Initial velocity fields are set to zero throughout the domain. Initial mass fraction fields define distinct compositions: in the pipe region, CH4 and N2 constitute the mixture, whereas the sleeve region contains O2 and N2. The membrane interface between pipe and sleeve utilizes semiPermeableBaffle boundary conditions for species mass fractions and velocity to enable mass transfer. Both membrane patches specify a permeability coefficient c=0.1, with adjacent patches sampling each other’s composition. Standard wall boundary conditions enforce no-slip velocity and wall functions for turbulence quantities. Pressure boundaries are configured using fixedFluxPressure for inlets and totalPressure for outlets.

The PIMPLE algorithm solves the flow, applying momentum prediction, 1 outer corrector, and 2 pressure correctors without non-orthogonal correction. Euler discretization handles time derivatives. Divergence schemes for convective fluxes are solved using the limitedLinear and limitedLinearV formats. Gradient and Laplacian terms utilize Gauss linear and Gauss linear orthogonal schemes, respectively. Solvers deploy PCG for pressure with DIC preconditioning and PBiCGStab for velocity, turbulence scalars, and enthalpy with DILU preconditioning, all with relative tolerance 0.1. Final iterations strictly enforce convergence via relTol=0.0.
