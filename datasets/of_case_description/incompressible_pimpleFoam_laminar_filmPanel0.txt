This simulation involves a laminar thin liquid film flow over an inclined panel geometry using the OpenFOAM solver `pimpleFoam`. The fluid is modelled as an incompressible Newtonian fluid with a kinematic viscosity (nu) of 1e-5 m²/s and a density (rhoInf) of 1.2 kg/m³. Gravitational acceleration is included and defined as a vector with a base magnitude of 9.81 m/s² applied at an inclination angle of 5 degrees in the yz-plane. The simulation is initialized with a relative pressure (p) field of zero everywhere and a velocity (U) field of zero everywhere.

The film dynamics are specifically resolved using kinematic thin-film modelling, where the film height (h_film) is initialized uniformly as zero internally but set to 1e-3 m at the inlet boundary. The film velocity (Uf_film) is initialized uniformly as zero internally but set to a uniform inlet velocity of -0.309 m/s in the y-direction. A dynamic contact angle boundary condition is applied at the film-wall interface with a constant contact angle of 100 degrees to model surface tension effects and wetting behavior. Shear stress at the film interface is modelled using a simple laminar friction law with a friction coefficient (Cf) set to zero.

The simulation employs an Euler scheme for time discretization. Spatial discretization uses Gauss linear schemes for gradients, a linearUpwind scheme with Gauss reconstruction for divergence (div(phi,U)), and Gauss linear corrected schemes for Laplacian terms. Pressure correction is solved using the GAMG algebraic multigrid solver with a tolerance of 0.02 and Gauss-Seidel smoothing, while the velocity field is solved using a smoothSolver with Gauss-Seidel smoothing and a tolerance of 1e-05. The PIMPLE algorithm is configured with one outer correction iteration and one pressure-velocity correction per time step, without non-orthogonal corrections or explicit relaxation (relaxation factor of 1 for velocity equations). No turbulence model is used, as simulationType is explicitly set to laminar.
