Perform a two-dimensional laminar simulation of a notched disc undergoing solid body rotation using the interIsoFoam solver for two-phase flow. The system contains immiscible fluids water (density 1000 kg/m³) and air (density 1 kg/m³), both modeled as inviscid Newtonian fluids with kinematic viscosity set to zero and no surface tension. Gravity acts vertically downward with acceleration (0, -9.81, 0) m/s².

Initial conditions define the entire domain filled with air (alpha.water = 0), zero velocity throughout, and zero modified pressure (p_rgh). Boundary conditions include a rim boundary with fixed zero volume fraction for water, zero-gradient conditions for velocity and pressure, and empty patches for the 2D front/back planes.

Numerical schemes use Euler time integration, Gauss linear corrected Laplacian terms, and limited linear divergence discretization for momentum. Interface capturing employs the isoAlpha reconstruction scheme with isosurface tolerance 1e-8 and surface cell tolerance 1e-8. Pressure-velocity coupling uses frozen flow mode, disabling momentum prediction and non-orthogonal corrections while maintaining constant reference pressure. No turbulence modeling is applied.
