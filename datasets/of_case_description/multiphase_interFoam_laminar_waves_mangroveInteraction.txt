Simulate wave interaction with mangroves in a two-phase (water-air) flow using the interFoam solver. The wave propagation is modelled with a Boussinesq wave on the left inlet (wave height 0.05 m, active absorption) and shallow water absorption on the right outlet. Mangroves are represented as porous regions (cell zone `c0`) using two source models: `multiphaseMangrovesSource` (coefficients a=0.01, N=560, Cd=1.52, Cm=1) for momentum interaction, and `multiphaseMangrovesTurbulenceModel` for k-epsilon turbulence closure (coefficients a=0.01, N=560, Cd=1.52, Ckp=1, Cep=3.5). The k-epsilon RANS model is active for turbulence. Both fluids are Newtonian, with water properties: nu=1e-06 m²/s, rho=1000 kg/m³; air properties: nu=1.48e-05 m²/s, rho=1 kg/m³. Surface tension coefficient is 0.07 N/m. Gravity acts in the negative z-direction (-9.81 m/s²). Initial conditions set water volume fraction, velocity, and modified pressure (p_rgh) to zero everywhere; turbulence kinetic energy (k=0.001 m²/s²) and dissipation rate (epsilon=0.0001 m²/s³) are initialised with near-zero values. Key solution settings use the PIMPLE algorithm (2 corrections, momentum prediction off). Discretisation employs an Euler scheme for time, Gauss linear for gradients, vanLeer for interface compression, and linearUpwind/upwind for convective terms. Solvers utilise PCG (with DIC/GAMG) for pressure and PBiCG (with DILU) for velocity and turbulence variables. Boundary conditions include `waveVelocity`, `waveAlpha` for wave inlets, pressure conditions at the top, wall functions (`epsilonWallFunction`, `kqRWallFunction`, `nutkWallFunction`) at the bottom, and slip on sides.
