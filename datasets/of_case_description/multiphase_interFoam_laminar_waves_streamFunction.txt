This OpenFOAM simulation employs the interFoam solver for modeling laminar wave dynamics using a stream function approach in a two-phase water-air system. Key configuration details include:

The computational setup utilizes the Volume of Fluid method with Newtonian fluid properties: water (density=1000 kg/m³, viscosity=1e-06 m²/s) and air (density=1 kg/m³, viscosity=1.48e-05 m²/s). Surface tension is modeled with a coefficient of 0.07 N/m, and gravitational acceleration is set to (0, 0, -9.81) m/s².

Discretization employs Euler time integration, Gauss linear gradients, and specialized divergence schemes: vanLeer for phase fraction (alpha) and linearUpwind for momentum (rhoPhi,U). The PIMPLE algorithm performs pressure-velocity coupling with 2 corrections per iteration and no momentum prediction.

Key solution settings:
1. Phase fraction (alpha.water) solver: 1 correction, 3 sub-cycles
2. Pressure (p_rgh) solver: PCG with DIC preconditioner (relative tolerance=0.1)
3. Velocity (U) solver: PBiCG with DILU preconditioner (relative tolerance=0.1)

Wave boundaries are defined using streamFunction at inlet (wave height=0.1517m, period=3.017s, mean velocity=2.0825m/s) with active absorption and ramp time of 6.034s. The outlet uses shallowWaterAbsorption model. Initial conditions set all velocity components, pressure, and water fraction to zero throughout the domain. Boundary conditions include wave-specific velocity inlets and fixedFluxPressure walls.
