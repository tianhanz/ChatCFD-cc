This OpenFOAM case simulates transonic flow around a NACA airfoil using the sonicFoam solver, incorporating compressibility effects and the Launder-Sharma k-epsilon turbulence model. The working fluid is air, modeled as a perfect gas with constant specific heat (Cp = 1005 J/kgK) and viscosity (mu = 1.8e-05 Pa·s).

Discretization schemes employ an Euler method for time derivatives. Gradients use cell-limited Gauss linear schemes, with velocity gradients specifically limited for stability. Divergence terms for momentum and energy utilize limited linear upwinding, while turbulence kinetic energy (k) and dissipation rate (epsilon) use first-order upwind schemes. Laplacian terms are treated with a Gauss linear limited corrected scheme, and surface normal gradients use a corrected scheme.

The PIMPLE algorithm is configured with 1 outer corrector and 2 inner correctors per time step, with no non-orthogonal correctors. Solvers are tailored: pressure uses PBiCGStab with DILU preconditioning and a tight tolerance. Velocity and internal energy solvers inherit the pressure solver setup but with looser tolerance, while k and epsilon solvers use even tighter tolerance. Density uses a diagonal solver due to explicit treatment.

Initial and boundary conditions are uniform. The inlet is set as a supersonicFreestream boundary defining pressure, temperature, and velocity. The outlet pressure uses a waveTransmissive condition, while outlet scalars (epsilon, k, T) and velocity use inletOutlet. Wall boundaries apply noSlip for velocity, zeroGradient for pressure and temperature, and specialized wall functions for turbulence quantities (nutkWallFunction, epsilonWallFunction, kqRWallFunction) and thermal diffusivity (alphatWallFunction). Symmetry planes are treated as empty boundaries. Turbulent kinetic energy and dissipation rate have uniform initial fields defined.
