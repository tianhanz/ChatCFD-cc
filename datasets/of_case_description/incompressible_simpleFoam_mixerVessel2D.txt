Perform a steady-state 2D incompressible turbulent flow simulation of a mixing vessel using the simpleFoam solver with the k-epsilon turbulence model. The setup includes a rotating impeller region defined via Multiple Reference Frame (MRF) with angular velocity 104.72 rad/s about the z-axis. The Newtonian fluid has kinematic viscosity 1e-05 m²/s. Initial conditions set velocity to zero, pressure to zero, turbulent kinetic energy to 1 m²/s², and dissipation rate to 20 m²/s³. Wall functions apply for turbulence quantities at rotor/stator boundaries using epsilonWallFunction for dissipation and kqRWallFunction/nutkWallFunction for kinetic energy and viscosity.

Numerical settings employ steady-state SIMPLE algorithm without non-orthogonal pressure corrections. Pressure solves using GAMG with tolerance 1e-08 and Gauss-Seidel smoothing. Velocity, k, and epsilon equations use smoothSolver with Gauss-Seidel iteration and 2 sweeps. Discretization schemes include limited linear differencing for momentum (limitedLinearV 1) and turbulence terms (limitedLinear 1), linear interpolation, and corrected surface normal gradients. Relaxation factors are 0.3 for pressure and 0.5 for velocity and turbulence equations.
