Simulate a 2-dimensional incompressible lid-driven cavity flow using the icoFoam solver without turbulence modeling. The top wall (movingWall) moves tangentially at a constant velocity of 1 m/s in the x-direction, while the bottom and side walls (fixedWalls) are stationary no-slip boundaries. Front and back planes are treated as empty boundaries. The fluid is Newtonian with a kinematic viscosity (nu) of 0.01 m²/s. Initial conditions set the velocity field uniformly to zero and the pressure field uniformly to zero.

Discretization employs the Euler scheme for time derivatives (ddt). Spatial gradients use the Gauss linear scheme. The divergence of the momentum flux (div(phi,U)) is discretized with Gauss linear. Laplacian and interpolation schemes use Gauss linear orthogonal and linear methods, respectively. Pressure gradients are computed using Gauss linear.

The PISO algorithm performs pressure-velocity coupling with 2 correction steps and no non-orthogonal corrections. Pressure is solved using the Preconditioned Conjugate Gradient (PCG) method with DIC preconditioning, relative tolerance of 0.05, and absolute tolerance of 1e-06. Velocity is solved using the smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel smoother and an absolute tolerance of 1e-05. A reference pressure cell is set to 0 with a reference value of 0. Boundary pressure is zero gradient on walls.
