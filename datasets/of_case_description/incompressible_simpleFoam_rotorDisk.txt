Simulating a steady-state incompressible flow around a rotating propeller disk using the simpleFoam solver with the k-Omega SST turbulence model. The fluid has constant kinematic viscosity (nu = 1.5e-05 m²/s) and is treated as Newtonian.

The simulation employs a rotating disk source term applied to the momentum equation within a specified cell zone ("rotatingZone") to model the propeller effect. This rotor has 3 blades, spins at 1000 RPM around the axis (0, 1, 0), and has its lift profile defined by blade sections and a lookup table for aerodynamic coefficients. The inlet velocity is fixed at (0 1 0) m/s. Discretization uses steady-state (ddtScheme), limited linear cell gradients for U, k, and omega (gradSchemes), linear interpolation, and a linearUpwind divergence scheme for momentum and turbulence transport. Pressure is solved with GAMG and Gauss-Seidel, while velocity and turbulence variables use smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel.

Initial and boundary conditions set uniform inlet velocity U = (0 1 0) m/s, turbulent kinetic energy k = 0.02 m²/s², and specific dissipation rate omega = 10 1/s. The pressure field starts uniformly at 0, with a zeroGradient condition at the inlet and fixedValue (0) at the outlet. The solver uses the SIMPLE algorithm with under-relaxation factors of 0.95 for U, k, and omega. Convergence is monitored with residual targets of 1e-4 for velocity and 1e-3 for pressure and turbulence quantities.
