This simulation employs the multiphaseInterFoam solver to model a 4-phase laminar dam break scenario involving water, oil, mercury, and air. The fluid phases are Newtonian, with densities of 1000 kg/m³ (water), 500 kg/m³ (oil), 13529 kg/m³ (mercury), and 1 kg/m³ (air). The kinematic viscosities are 1e-06 m²/s (water), 1e-06 m²/s (oil), 1.125e-07 m²/s (mercury), and 1.48e-05 m²/s (air). A constant surface tension coefficient of 0.07 N/m applies between all phase pairs. Gravity acts vertically with a magnitude of 9.81 m/s². The initial velocity field is zero everywhere. The initial volume fraction fields are uniformly zero except at the 'atmosphere' boundary where air is initialized to 1 (right wall) or 0 (other boundaries). The initial modified pressure (p_rgh) field is uniformly zero. A fixed contact angle of 90 degrees is defined for all phase pairs on the walls.

The discretization uses an Euler scheme for time derivatives. Gradients are computed using Gauss linear schemes. Divergence terms are handled with Gauss upwind for rhoPhi*U, Gauss vanLeer for phi*alpha (interface capturing), and Gauss linear for other div terms. Laplacian schemes use Gauss linear corrected. Interpolation is linear, and surface normal gradients use a corrected scheme.

Solution control (fvSolution) applies 4 sub-cycles for alpha equations to maintain interface sharpness. The PIMPLE algorithm is configured with momentum prediction off, 2 pressure-velocity correctors, and no non-orthogonal correctors. Solvers utilize GAMG (algebraic multigrid) with Gauss-Seidel smoother and a tolerance of 1e-07 for p_rgh, and smoothSolver with Gauss-Seidel and tolerance 1e-09 for U. Relaxation factors are set to 1 for velocity equations. Boundaries use no-slip for walls on U, fixedFluxPressure for walls on p_rgh, and totalPressure for atmosphere on p_rgh. Volume fraction boundaries include zeroGradient on walls and inletOutlet on atmosphere.
