Employ the pimpleFoam solver to conduct a laminar flow simulation involving a moving cone. The fluid is Newtonian with a kinematic viscosity of 1e-05 m²/s. The mesh deforms dynamically to accommodate the cone's motion, utilizing the velocityComponentLaplacian motion solver constrained to the x-direction. Anisotropic diffusivity is applied, significantly higher in the y-direction compared to x and z.

Initial velocity is zero throughout the domain. The cone boundary ("movingWall") moves at a constant velocity of 1 m/s in the x-direction, specified via the pointMotionUx field and enforced through boundary conditions. Other walls are either fixed walls ("fixedWall", "farField") with no-slip velocity conditions or treated as wedges ("back", "front"). Pressure uses zeroGradient on most boundaries except the "left" boundary, which employs a totalPressure condition matching the initial internal pressure field of 0.

Discretization employs the Euler scheme for time derivatives, Gauss linear for gradients, and linear interpolation. The divergence term for velocity and flux (div(phi,U)) uses Gauss linear. Pressure Laplacian uses the Gauss linear corrected scheme. Solution settings involve 2 outer correctors and 1 corrector within the PIMPLE loop, with pressure-Velocity coupling activated via the correctPhi flag. The pressure equation employs a GAMG solver with a relative tolerance of 0.01, while velocity uses a smoothSolver (symGaussSeidel smoother) with a relative tolerance of 0.1. No under-relaxation is applied to velocity equations.
