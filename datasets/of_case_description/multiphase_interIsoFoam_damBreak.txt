This case simulates a 2D dam break scenario using the interIsoFoam solver for immiscible incompressible fluids under laminar flow conditions. The model considers water and air as Newtonian fluids, with water having a density of 1000 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s, while air has a density of 1 kg/m³ and viscosity of 1.48e-05 m²/s. Surface tension is modeled with a coefficient of 0.07 N/m. Gravity acts vertically downward at 9.81 m/s².

Initially, the water volume fraction (alpha.water) and velocity field are uniformly zero throughout the domain. Modified pressure (p_rgh) is initially zero. Boundary conditions include no-slip walls (fixedValue velocity and zeroGradient alpha.water) and an atmospheric boundary (pressureInletOutletVelocity for U, inletOutlet for alpha.water with zero inlet value, and totalPressure for p_rgh).

The discretization employs Euler time stepping, Gauss linear gradients, limitedLinearV divergence for momentum, and linear corrected Laplacian schemes. For alpha.water, the isoAlpha reconstruction scheme tracks the interface with clipping enabled and specified tolerances. The pressure solver uses GAMG with DICGaussSeidel smoothing, while velocity employs PBiCGStab with DILU preconditioner. The PIMPLE algorithm performs 3 pressure corrections per outer iteration without momentum prediction or non-orthogonal correction.
