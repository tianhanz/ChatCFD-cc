Employing the sonicFoam solver to conduct a 2D laminar compressible flow simulation over a forward-facing step geometry. The simulation assumes inviscid-like conditions due to zero dynamic viscosity in the thermophysical model while maintaining perfect gas behavior. The domain utilizes symmetry boundary conditions on both the top and bottom walls and defines an obstacle surface with no-slip and zero-pressure gradient conditions.

A wave-transmissive boundary condition is applied at the outlet to mitigate pressure wave reflections. Initial uniform fields are set: pressure (p) at 1 Pa, velocity (U) at (3 0 0) m/s, and temperature (T) at 1 K. The thermophysical model uses constant properties with specific heat capacity (Cp) of 2.5 J/kg·K and a large molecular weight of 11640.3 g/mol.

The PIMPLE algorithm is configured with 2 outer correctors and 1 inner corrector per time step, handling pressure-velocity coupling without non-orthogonal corrections. Discretization employs Euler time differencing and Gauss upwind scheme for convective terms of velocity. Pressure equations are solved using the smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel smoother, whereas density equations use a PCG solver with DIC preconditioner. Tolerances are set at 1e-6 for pressure and 1e-5 for velocity and energy fields.
