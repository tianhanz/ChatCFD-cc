Using the interFoam solver to conduct a laminar simulation of multi-paddle flap-type wave generation in a two-phase water-air system. The case involves flap-type wave motion initiated at the left boundary wall with gravity acting vertically downward. Both phases are Newtonian fluids with water density 1000 kg/m³, viscosity 1e-06 m²/s, and air density 1 kg/m³, viscosity 1.48e-05 m²/s, incorporating surface tension (sigma=0.07 N/m).

The computational domain uses dynamic mesh motion handled by a displacementLaplacian solver with inverseDistance diffusivity relative to the left boundary. Wave properties include 4 paddles, 0.06 m wave height, 2.0 s period, and 45° wave angle with 2.0 s ramp-up time. Initial conditions set uniform zero velocity, zero pseudo-pressure (p_rgh), and zero water volume fraction throughout.

Discretization employs Euler time scheme, Gauss linear gradients, and specific divergence schemes: vanLeer for phase fraction (alpha), interfaceCompression for phirb term, and linearUpwind for velocity. Pressure-velocity coupling uses PIMPLE algorithm with 3 correctors and no non-orthogonal correction. Solvers include GAMG for pressure/pressure-correction (DICGaussSeidel smoother, 1e-7 tolerance) and PBiCGStab for velocity (DILU preconditioner, 1e-6 tolerance). Boundary conditions feature movingWallVelocity at wave-generating wall, waveVelocity at opposing boundary, slip conditions at side walls, and pressureInletOutletVelocity at top.
