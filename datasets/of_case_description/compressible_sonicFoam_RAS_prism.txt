This OpenFOAM simulation utilizes the `sonicFoam` solver to model steady, compressible, turbulent flow at transonic/supersonic speeds over a prism-shaped obstacle. The fluid is air, modeled as a perfect gas with constant thermophysical properties (molecular weight = 28.9 g/mol, Cp = 1005 J/kg/K, dynamic viscosity = 1.8e-05 Pa·s). The turbulence is modeled using the k-Epsilon (RANS) approach.

The initial conditions set a uniform flow field throughout the domain: velocity = (650, 0, 0) m/s, pressure = 100000 Pa, temperature = 300 K, turbulent kinetic energy (k) = 1000 m²/s², turbulent dissipation rate (epsilon) = 266000 m²/s³. Boundary conditions include a fixed pressure and velocity `inlet`, a `waveTransmissive` pressure condition at the `outlet` to handle pressure waves, `supersonicFreestream` conditions on the `bottomWall` and `topWall` to maintain supersonic inflow properties, and a `noSlip` velocity condition with corresponding wall functions (e.g., `kqRWallFunction`, `epsilonWallFunction`, `nutkWallFunction`) on the `prismWall` itself. Other walls and the prism use `zeroGradient` pressure.

Spatial discretization employs the Gauss theorem. Gradient terms use `Gauss linear`. Divergence schemes are primarily `limitedLinear` with a limiter value of 1 for velocity, internal energy, and pressure-related fluxes. Turbulence terms (`k` and `epsilon`) use an `upwind` scheme. The default `Gauss linear corrected` scheme is used for Laplacians. Time integration uses the Euler scheme (`Euler`). For solving, density fields use a diagonal solver. Pressure is solved using a `smoothSolver` (smoother: `symGaussSeidel`) with an absolute tolerance of 1e-08. Velocity, internal energy, and Reynolds stress fields share the pressure solver configuration but use a tolerance of 1e-05. Turbulence fields (`k` and `epsilon`) also inherit the pressure solver setup but use a stricter tolerance of 1e-08. The PIMPLE algorithm is configured for 2 outer correctors and 1 pressure corrector, with no non-orthogonal correction.
