This OpenFOAM case models atmospheric flow around turbine sites using the steady-state RANS solver `simpleFoam`. The k-epsilon model simulates turbulence, with modified coefficients optimized for atmospheric boundary layers: Cμ = 0.09, C₁ = 1.44, C₂ = 1.92, and σ_ε = 1.11. Newtonian fluid properties apply, with kinematic viscosity ν = 1.5e⁻⁵ m²/s.

The setup incorporates two actuation disks representing turbines. Disk 1 uses the Froude variant with thrust coefficient Ct = 0.58 and power coefficient Cp = 0.386. Disk 2 has Ct = 0.58 and Cp = 0.53. Both specify disk areas and orientations to simulate momentum sources/sinks.

Key discretization schemes:  
- Convection terms for velocity (U), turbulent kinetic energy (k), and dissipation rate (ε) use **bounded Gauss upwind**.  
- Gradient terms apply **Gauss linear**.  
- Laplacian terms use **Gauss linear limited corrected** with coefficient 0.33.  
- Pressure-velocity coupling employs the **SIMPLE** algorithm with **0 non-orthogonal correctors**.  

Solver and relaxation settings:  
- **Pressure (p)**: GAMG solver with Gauss-Seidel smoother, tolerance 1e⁻⁷, relaxation 0.3.  
- **Velocity (U)**, **k**, and **ε**: smoothSolver with Gauss-Seidel, tolerance 1e⁻⁸, relaxation 0.7.  
- **Residual controls**: p ≤ 1e⁻³, U ≤ 1e⁻⁴, k/ε ≤ 1e⁻⁴.  

Boundary conditions include:  
- Atmospheric inlet profiles for U, k, and ε with roughness modeling.  
- Wall functions for terrain (epsilonWallFunction for ε, kqRWallFunction for k).  
- InletOutlet conditions at outlets.  
- Zero pressure gradient at terrain/ground.  

The domain initializes uniform fields for pressure, velocity, k, and ε from include files, with nut initialized as zero.
