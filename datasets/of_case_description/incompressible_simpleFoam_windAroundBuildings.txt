This simulation employs the simpleFoam solver to simulate steady-state turbulent wind flow around buildings using the kEpsilon turbulence model. The fluid is Newtonian with a kinematic viscosity of 1.5e-05 m²/s. The inlet boundary has a fixed velocity of 10 m/s in the x-direction and zero in other components, along with specified turbulent kinetic energy (k = 1.5 m²/s²) and turbulent dissipation rate (ε = 0.03 m²/s³). Outlet boundaries use a constant total pressure condition for pressure and a pressure-inlet-outlet condition for velocity. Wall boundaries enforce no-slip conditions for velocity and wall functions for turbulence variables (k, ε, nut). The pressure solver uses a GAMG algorithm with Gauss-Seidel smoothing and relaxation factor 0.3. Velocity and turbulence fields (U, k, ε) are solved using a smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel smoothing and relaxation factor 0.7. Residual tolerances of 1e-4 are targeted for pressure, velocity, and turbulence fields. Spatial discretization employs Gauss linear schemes for gradients, with cell limiting applied for U, k, and ε gradients. Momentum advection uses the bounded linearUpwind limited scheme, while turbulence advection uses a bounded limitedLinear scheme.
