This simulation employs the twoPhaseEulerFoam solver to model laminar bubbly flow in a vertical column using OpenFOAM. It examines air bubbles rising through a stationary water phase under gravity, with initial air volume fraction set to zero except at the inlet where it is 0.5. Surface tension coefficient is 0.07 N/m.  

The solver settings use the PIMPLE algorithm with 3 outer correctors and 1 pressure corrector. Discretization applies the vanLeer scheme for air fraction advection and limitedLinear scheme for momentum equations. Pressure is solved via GAMG with DIC smoother.  

Thermophysical properties define water as an incompressible fluid (density 1027 kg/m³, constant viscosity) and air as a perfect gas. Drag between phases is modeled via Schiller-Naumann correlations without turbulence modeling. Bubble diameter is fixed at 3 mm for air and 0.1 mm for water. Virtual mass forces are included with coefficient 0.5.  

Boundary conditions specify fixed inlet velocities (air: y-direction 0.1 m/s, water: stationary), zero-gradient walls, and pressure-driven outlet conditions. Gravity acts vertically downward at 9.81 m/s². The solution relaxes equations fully without under-relaxation.
