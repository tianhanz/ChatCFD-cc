Simulating planar Poiseuille flow using the Maxwell viscoelastic model with pimpleFoam solver in laminar conditions. The setup represents 2D flow between parallel plates driven by a constant body force. Spatial discretization employs second-order schemes: Gauss linear for gradients, vanAlbada divergence for stress transport, and linear upwinding for momentum convection. Temporal discretization uses the backward scheme. The pressure-velocity coupling utilizes the PIMPLE algorithm with 15 outer loops and 3 pressure correctors, momentum prediction disabled. Solvers configure GAMG with DIC smoother for pressure, and symGaussSeidel smoother for velocity/stress fields. The Maxwell model parameters are set to kinematic viscosity nuM = 1 and relaxation time lambda = 5. A steady momentum source (5 0 0) is activated from time zero in specific volume mode. Initial conditions define stationary fluid with zero pressure, zero velocity, and zero viscoelastic stress fields throughout the domain. Wall boundaries enforce no-slip velocity conditions and zero stress/pressure gradients.
