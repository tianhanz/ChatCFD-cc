Using the interFoam solver, conduct a 2-dimensional laminar simulation of cnoidal wave propagation in a multiphase system comprising water and air. Both phases are Newtonian fluids: water has a kinematic viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s and density of 1000 kg/m³, while air has a kinematic viscosity of 1.48e-05 m²/s and density of 1 kg/m³. The surface tension coefficient is 0.07 N/m.

The simulation employs the cnoidal wave model at the inlet boundary with a wave height of 0.1 meters and a wave period of 4 seconds. Active wave absorption is enabled at the inlet and outlet. Initial conditions set the water volume fraction (alpha.water) and velocity vector field uniformly to zero throughout the domain. The modified pressure field (p_rgh) also starts uniformly at zero.

The temporal discretization uses the Euler scheme. Spatial discretization applies Gauss linear schemes for gradients and orthogonal Laplacians. Divergence schemes are Gauss linearUpwind for momentum and Gauss vanLeer for phase fraction. Velocity is solved using preconditioned biconjugate gradient with diagonal incomplete LU decomposition, achieving convergence tolerance to 1e-6. Pressure fields are solved using preconditioned conjugate gradient with diagonal incomplete Cholesky, with tolerance to 1e-6 and relative tolerance 0.1 for p_rgh. Pressure-velocity coupling uses PIMPLE with 2 momentum corrections and no non-orthogonal correction.
