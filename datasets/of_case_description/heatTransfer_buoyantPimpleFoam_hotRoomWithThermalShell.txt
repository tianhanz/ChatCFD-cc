This OpenFOAM simulation models buoyancy-driven turbulent airflow coupled with radiation heat transfer and conjugate heat conduction in a ceiling structure within a heated room, using the `buoyantPimpleFoam` solver with the `kEpsilon` RANS turbulence model. Radiation is modeled using the `fvDOM` (Discrete Ordinates Method) with constant absorption/emission coefficients. The problem includes gravity acting vertically downward, an initial uniform temperature field of 300 K throughout the domain, and an initial fluid at rest. Key boundaries consist of a fixed-value hot floor surface maintained at 700 K, a ceiling modeled as a thin thermal shell undergoing conduction (with specified material properties: thermal conductivity 200 W/m/K, density 1000 kg/m³, specific heat 600 J/kg/K), and insulated fixed walls with no-slip velocity conditions. The numerical approach employs the PIMPLE algorithm for pressure-velocity coupling with momentum prediction enabled. Discretization utilizes the Euler scheme for time derivatives, linear Gauss methods for gradients and Laplacians (corrected), and predominantly upwind divergence schemes for convection terms of flow variables (U, h, e, k, epsilon, R), while linear Gauss divergence is used for other scalars. Solver settings include PCG with DIC preconditioner for density/pressure-related equations (p_rgh tolerance 1e-8) and PBiCGStab with DILU preconditioner for velocity, energy, and turbulence variables (tolerance 1e-6, relTol 0.1). The ceiling thermal shell utilizes a coupled conduction-radiation model at its interface.
