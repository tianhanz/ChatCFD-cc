Utilize the potentialFoam solver to perform a steady-state simulation of inviscid, incompressible potential flow past a cylinder. The simulation solves the Laplace equation for velocity potential to obtain an irrotational velocity field.

An inlet boundary condition at the left domain face imposes a uniform freestream velocity of (1 0 0) m/s. Top and bottom boundaries use symmetry plane conditions. The right boundary employs a zero-gradient condition for pressure and velocity. Pressure is fixed at 0 on the right face, while its gradient is set to zero at the inlet. The cylinder surface itself is treated as a symmetry boundary for both pressure and velocity fields. Internal fields are initialized uniformly with zero pressure and zero velocity.

The numerical approach employs the steady-state time scheme. Gradient terms are discretized using the least squares method. Velocity flux divergence uses the bounded Gauss linear scheme. Laplacian terms, relevant for solving the potential equation, use Gauss linear corrected interpolation. Surface normal gradient discretization applies the corrected scheme to accommodate potential mesh non-orthogonality.

Solution of the primary scalar potential field (`Phi`) and the pressure field (`p`) leverages the Geometric Algebraic Multi-Grid (GAMG) linear solver. A Diagonal Incomplete-Cholesky (DIC) smoother is used, with solver tolerances set to an absolute tolerance of 1e-06 and a relative tolerance of 0.01. Up to 3 non-orthogonal correction steps are performed per solver iteration to enhance solution stability and accuracy on the mesh.
