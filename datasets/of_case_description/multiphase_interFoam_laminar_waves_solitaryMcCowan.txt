The laminar wave simulation employs the interFoam solver to model a solitary McCowan wave in a multiphase system with water and air. Both fluids are Newtonian, with water having a kinematic viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s and density of 1000 kg/m³, while air has a kinematic viscosity of 1.48e-05 m²/s and density of 1 kg/m³. Surface tension is set to 0.07 N/m, and gravity acts downward at -9.81 m/s². Initially, the domain is filled with air (alpha.water = 0), with zero velocity and modified pressure (p_rgh) everywhere. Boundary conditions include wave-specific velocity and pressure settings for inlet/outlet boundaries, no-slip walls, and symmetric sides. The PIMPLE algorithm uses two pressure-velocity correctors without momentum prediction, and linear discretization schemes are applied for gradients and Laplace terms, while divergence schemes use vanLeer for interface sharpness and linearUpwind for momentum stability. Solvers utilize PCG/GAMG for pressure with DIC/DILU preconditioning and PBiCG for velocity.
