The buoyantCavity case simulates buoyancy-driven flow within an enclosed cavity under steady-state conditions using the buoyantSimpleFoam solver. Gravity acts downward with a magnitude of 9.81 m/s². Thermal buoyancy forces arise due to differentially heated vertical walls: one maintained at 307.75 K (hot) and the opposite at 288.15 K (cold), while top/bottom/front/back walls are adiabatic. All walls enforce a no-slip velocity condition. Turbulence is modeled using the k-Omega SST closure.

The thermophysical model employs a perfect gas equation of state with constant properties: molar mass 28.96 g/mol, dynamic viscosity 1.831e-05 Pa·s, Prandtl number 0.705, and specific heat capacity 1004.4 J/kg·K.

Discretization uses a steady-state formulation for time derivatives (ddtScheme: steadyState). Gradients use Gauss linear schemes. Divergence schemes for momentum (phi,U), energy (phi,h), and turbulence variables (phi,k, phi,epsilon, phi,omega) apply bounded Gauss limitedLinear with a flux limiter coefficient of 0.2. Laplacian and surface normal gradients use orthogonal corrections. Wall distance calculation uses the meshWave method.

Solution algorithms utilize the SIMPLE approach without momentum prediction and with zero non-orthogonal corrections. Solver settings are: pressure (p_rgh) employs GAMG with DICGaussSeidel smoothing, tolerance 1e-7; velocity (U), enthalpy (h), and turbulence scalars (k, epsilon, omega) use PBiCGStab with DILU preconditioning, tolerance 1e-8. Relaxation factors are: pressure 0.7, velocity and enthalpy 0.3, turbulence fields 0.7. Residual convergence targets are: p_rgh, U, h at 1e-4; k, epsilon, omega at 1e-3.

Initial conditions are: pressure (p, p_rgh) uniform at 1e5 Pa; velocity zero everywhere; temperature 293 K; turbulence fields initialized as k=3.75e-4 m²/s², epsilon=4e-6 m²/s³, omega=0.12 s⁻¹; turbulent viscosity and thermal diffusivity fields (nut, alphat) start as zero with wall function boundaries.
