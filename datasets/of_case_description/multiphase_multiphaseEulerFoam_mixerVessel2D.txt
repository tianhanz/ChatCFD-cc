This simulation models a 2D four-phase fluid mixing process within a vessel using the `multiphaseEulerFoam` solver. It involves water, oil, mercury, and air phases. Initial volume fractions for each phase are set uniformly to 0.25 across the domain. Velocity and pressure fields are initialized to zero.

Gravity is absent. The rotor zone spins at a constant angular velocity around the z-axis via the Multiple Reference Frame (MRF) model. Laminar flow is assumed.

For the discretization: Euler scheme is used for time derivatives. Gradients employ Gauss linear interpolation. Van Leer scheme is used for divergence terms involving phase fractions (`div(phi,alpha.*)`, `div(phir,alpha.*,alpha.*)`), and the limitedLinearV scheme (with coefficient 1) is used for velocity divergences (`div(alphaPhi.*,U.*)`, `div(phi.*,U.*)`). Laplacian terms use Gauss linear corrected, while snGrad uses the corrected scheme.

Solution settings include: 1 PIMPLE outer corrector and 3 pressure correctors with no non-orthogonal correction. Solver tolerances are specified: GAMG for `p_rgh` (relTol 0.01, tolerance 1e-7), PCG for `p_rghFinal` (tolerance 1e-7), and smoothSolver (GaussSeidel sweeper) for velocities `U` (relTol 0.1, tolerance 1e-8). Phase fraction equations use 2 sub-cycles. Drag forces between phases utilize a blended SchillerNaumann model with residual parameters. Boundary conditions enforce no-slip on the rotating rotor and stationary stator walls for velocity, zero pressure gradient, and zero slip pressure condition. Front and back boundaries are empty.
