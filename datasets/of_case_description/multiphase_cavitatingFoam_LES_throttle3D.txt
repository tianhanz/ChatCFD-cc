This case simulates three-dimensional cavitating flow through a throttle geometry using the cavitatingFoam solver with a k-equation LES turbulence model. The fluid consists of vapor and liquid water phases, both modeled as Newtonian fluids. The liquid water has a density of 830 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity of 7.831e-06 m²/s, while vapor has a density of 0.14 kg/m³ and viscosity of 4.252e-05 m²/s. Barotropic compressibility is modeled linearly with a saturation pressure of 4500 Pa. Initial conditions set pressure uniformly at 300 bar and density at 845 kg/m³ throughout, with zero velocity and uniform turbulent kinetic energy of 10 m²/s². Boundary conditions include total pressure at inlet (300 bar) and fixed pressure at outlet (100 bar), with no-slip walls and zero-gradient conditions for other variables.

Discretization uses the Euler scheme for time derivatives, linear interpolation, and Gauss linear corrected laplacians. Specific divergence schemes include vanLeer for density and LUST for velocity and turbulent kinetic energy. The PIMPLE algorithm employs 3 corrector steps with no non-orthogonal correction. Pressure is solved using GAMG with a smoother tolerance of 0.1, while other variables use smoothSolver with Gauss-Seidel smoothing and relative tolerance of 0.1. All equations have a relaxation factor of 1.
