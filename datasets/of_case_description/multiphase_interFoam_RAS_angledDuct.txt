Simulating multiphase turbulent flow through an angled duct using the interFoam solver with the k-epsilon turbulence model. The simulation involves two Newtonian fluids: water (density 1000 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1e-06 m²/s) and air (density 1 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1.48e-05 m²/s), with a surface tension coefficient of 0.07 N/m.

The domain initial conditions set the velocity (U) and modified pressure (p_rgh) fields uniformly to zero. The volume fraction of water (alpha.water) is also uniformly zero initially. At the inlet, the volume fraction of water is fixed at 1 (indicating pure water inflow), and a constant mass flow rate of 0.1 kg/s is prescribed for velocity. Boundary conditions enforce slip at the porosity wall, while other walls are no-slip. Pressure boundary conditions use fixedFluxPressure at walls and inlet, and fixedValue at the outlet. Turbulence boundary conditions apply wall functions for k and epsilon on walls, with specified turbulent intensity and mixing length at the inlet.

A Darcy-Forchheimer explicit porosity source term models resistance within a defined cell zone. Gravity acts in the negative y-direction with magnitude 9.81 m/s².

Key discretization schemes include:
- Time derivative: Euler.
- Gradient: Gauss linear.
- Divergence: Upwind for momentum and turbulence transport; vanLeer for phase fraction (alpha).
- Laplacian: Gauss linear corrected.

The PIMPLE algorithm configures one outer corrector and three pressure correctors per time step, without momentum prediction or non-orthogonal correctors. Solver settings vary per equation: smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel smoother for alpha and turbulence equations; GAMG with GaussSeidel smoother for pressure (p_rgh); PCG for pressure correction (pcorr). Relaxation factors of 1 are applied across all equations.
