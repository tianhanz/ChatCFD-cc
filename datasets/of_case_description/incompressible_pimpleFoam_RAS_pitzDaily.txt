We aim to simulate transient turbulent flow over a backward-facing step (PitzDaily case) using the pimpleFoam solver with the k-epsilon RANS model for turbulence closure. The fluid is Newtonian with kinematic viscosity ν = 1e-05 m²/s. Initially, the velocity field is uniformly zero except at the inlet boundary where it is fixed at (10, 0, 0) m/s. Pressure starts uniformly at zero, with zero gradient at inlet and fixed zero value at outlet. Turbulent kinetic energy (k) is initialized uniformly at 0.375 m²/s² and turbulent dissipation rate (ε) at 14.855 m²/s³, both fixed at inlet. Wall boundaries implement wall functions for k (kqRWallFunction), ε (epsilonWallFunction), and turbulent viscosity (nutkWallFunction).  
Discretization uses Euler scheme for time derivatives, Gauss linear for gradients, and linear corrected for Laplacian terms. The velocity divergence employs a linearUpwind scheme, while turbulence variables use an upwind convective discretization. Pressure is solved with GAMG and DICGaussSeidel smoother at relative tolerance 0.01. Velocity and turbulence equations are solved via smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel smoother at relative tolerance 0.1. The PIMPLE algorithm uses 2 corrector steps without non-orthogonal corrections.
