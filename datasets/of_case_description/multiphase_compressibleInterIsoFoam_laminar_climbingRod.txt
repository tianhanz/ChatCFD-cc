This OpenFOAM simulation uses the `compressibleInterIsoFoam` solver to model a two-phase (liquid and air) laminar flow exhibiting viscoelastic behavior. The key phenomenon studied is the climbing of a viscoelastic liquid film up a vertically oriented rotating rod driven by normal stress differences, with air as the ambient phase. Gravity acts vertically downward.

The liquid phase is modeled using a laminar Maxwell viscoelastic constitutive equation, specified within its turbulence properties. An additional stabilization model limits the liquid stress (`sigma.liquid`) near phase interfaces to prevent numerical instabilities. Surface tension acts between the liquid and air. Thermodynamic properties (density, specific heat, viscosity) for both phases are constant. The initial volume fraction of liquid (`alpha.liquid`) is zero throughout the domain, while temperature (`T`) and pressure fields (`p`, `p_rgh`) are initially uniform. The velocity field (`U`) appears stationary initially.

The rod boundary applies a rotating wall velocity condition with a constant angular speed. Vessel walls are no-slip. The atmosphere boundary is modeled using a combination of inlet/outlet conditions for scalars and pressure-driven velocity.

The solver employs the PIMPLE algorithm for pressure-velocity coupling, configured with 1 outer correction and 2 pressure correctors per timestep, without momentum prediction and non-orthogonal correction. Discretization uses second-order accurate schemes: Euler for temporal derivatives, Gauss linear for gradients and the laplacian, Gauss vanLeer for divergence of alpha, Gauss linearUpwindV for momentum, and Gauss upwind for other scalars. Linear interpolation and corrected surface normal gradients are applied. Specific solvers (PCG with DIC for pressure, PBiCGStab with DILU for velocity/stress) and tolerances are defined for solving the matrix systems. Relaxation factors are disabled.
