Conducting a 2D steady-state simulation of incompressible flow over a symmetric bump geometry using simpleFoam without turbulence modeling. The fluid is Newtonian with kinematic viscosity set to 2.31e-5 m²/s. Initial conditions feature uniform inlet velocity of 69.44 m/s in the streamwise direction and zero pressure field. Boundary conditions include fixed inlet velocity, symmetry planes on top and sides, no-slip at the bump surface, outlet pressure set to zero, and empty front/back patches for 2D treatment. The SIMPLE algorithm resolves pressure-velocity coupling consistently with under-relaxation factors of 0.9 for velocity and 0.7 for other variables. Discretization utilizes second-order schemes: Gauss linear for gradients, bounded Gauss linearUpwind for velocity divergence, Gauss linear corrected for Laplacians, and linear interpolation. Linear solvers employ GAMG with DICGaussSeidel smoother for pressure and PBiCGStab with DILU preconditioner for velocity, both with tight convergence tolerances of 1e-8 absolute and 0 relative tolerance. Convergence is monitored via residual controls at 1e-5 for all fields.
