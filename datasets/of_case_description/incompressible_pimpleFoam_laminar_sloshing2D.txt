The laminar/sloshing2D case simulates two-dimensional free surface sloshing phenomena using the pimpleFoam solver. This case models a liquid with Newtonian properties (kinematic viscosity nu = 0 m²/s, density rho = 1 kg/m³ and surface tension coefficient sigma = 0.1 N/m) under gravity (g = (0, -1, 0) m/s²). It employs an interface tracking approach for the free surface dynamics. The dynamic mesh is governed by a velocity Laplacian motion solver with surface normals corrected on 'left' and 'right' patches, and the 'top' patch defined as the free surface boundary.

The simulation utilizes an implicit backward temporal scheme for time derivatives. Spatial discretization employs linear interpolation and Gauss linear divergence for the velocity flux. Pressure gradients use Gauss linear schemes, with linear corrected Gauss schemes for Laplacian terms and corrected surface normal gradients. Pressure-velocity coupling uses the PIMPLE algorithm with 12 outer correctors and 3 correctors per outer iteration. Mesh motion is enabled during outer iterations to accommodate interface deformation.

Solver settings are customized: the pressure (p) is solved with the GAMG method (Geometric Algebraic Multigrid) with no relaxation and tolerance of 1e-8. Velocity (U) uses the smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel smoother and tolerance of 1e-9. The free surface pressure at the 'top' boundary is fixed at 0 Pa, while velocity at the 'top' uses 'freeSurfaceVelocity'. Both velocity and pressure employ a zero-gradient condition at other walls ('bottom', 'left', 'right'). Initial conditions assume uniform zero relative pressure and zero velocity throughout the domain.
