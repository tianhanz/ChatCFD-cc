Using the icoFoam solver to conduct a three-dimensional simulation of an incompressible, laminar lid-driven cavity flow. The flow domain represents a cavity where the top wall (movingWall) translates tangentially at a constant velocity of (1, 0, 0) m/s. The other three walls (fixedWalls) enforce a no-slip condition, and the frontAndBack boundaries are treated as empty to represent a quasi-two-dimensional geometry. The working fluid is Newtonian with a constant kinematic viscosity (nu) of 0.01 m²/s.

Initial conditions set both velocity (U) and modified pressure (p) uniformly to zero throughout the entire domain. For spatial discretization, gradients use Gauss linear, velocity divergence uses Gauss linear, Laplacians use Gauss linear corrected, and surface-normal gradients use corrected schemes. The Euler scheme handles temporal discretization. Solver settings employ a smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel smoother for the velocity (U) field. For the pressure (p) field, a Preconditioned Conjugate Gradient (PCG) solver with Diagonal Incomplete Cholesky (DIC) preconditioner is used, applying relative tolerance control during iterations. The transient coupling is handled via the PISO algorithm with 2 pressure correctors and no non-orthogonal correction.
