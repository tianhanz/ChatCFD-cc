Simulating a rotating propeller in an incompressible turbulent flow using the pimpleFoam solver with k-epsilon turbulence modeling. The propeller rotates at 158 rad/s around the y-axis (axis 0 1 0) via dynamic mesh motion applied to the inner cylinder. Initial flow velocity is stationary (0 m/s), with a uniform inlet velocity of 5 m/s directed along the negative y-axis and zero-pressure outlet. Turbulence fields k and epsilon initialize uniformly at 0.06 m²/s² and 0.0495 m²/s³ respectively, using wall functions for boundaries. Newtonian fluid properties include a constant kinematic viscosity of 1e-6 m²/s.  

Numerical schemes apply Euler time integration, cell-limited Gauss linear gradient discretization (limited to prevent overshoots), and linear upwind convection for momentum. Turbulence transport uses upwind convection for stability. Pressure solution employs GAMG with geometric-algebraic multigrid, while velocity and turbulence equations use symmetric Gauss-Seidel smoothing. The PIMPLE algorithm uses 2 outer correctors and 1 momentum corrector per timestep with no non-orthogonal correction. Relaxation factors are set to 1 for all equations.
