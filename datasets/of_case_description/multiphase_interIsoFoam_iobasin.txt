The iobasin case employs the interIsoFoam solver for simulating laminar multiphase flow with immiscible water and air phases in an idealized basin configuration. Both phases are modeled as Newtonian fluids, where water has a density of 1000 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s, while air has a density of 1 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity of 1.48e-05 m²/s, with a surface tension coefficient of 0.07 N/m.

Initial conditions set a uniform velocity field of (0.1 0 0) m/s throughout the domain. The water volume fraction (alpha.water) is initialized uniformly as zero, except at the inlet boundary where it is fixed at unity (pure water). The modified pressure field (p_rgh) is initialized uniformly at zero. Gravity is defined with a constant acceleration vector (0 0 -9.81) m/s².

Boundary conditions include:
- A volumetric flow rate inlet for velocity (0.1 m³/s) with fixed alpha.water (1) for water inflow.
- Slip walls for velocity and zero gradient for alpha.water.
- Flow rate outlet for velocity.
- Atmosphere boundary with inletOutlet condition for alpha.water and totalPressure for p_rgh.

Key numerical settings use:
- The isoAlpha scheme for interface reconstruction.
- Euler time differencing.
- Gauss linear corrected Laplacian schemes.
- Limited linear divergence scheme for momentum (div(rhoPhi,U)).
- Upwind divergence for turbulence quantities and vanLeer for scalar transport.

The pressure-velocity coupling employs the PIMPLE algorithm with 2 correctors without momentum prediction. Pressure is solved using GAMG with Gauss-Seidel smoother and relative tolerance of 0.01. Velocity and scalar transport equations are solved via a smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel sweeps and a relative tolerance of 0.1. No turbulence model is active.
