This simulation utilizes the interCondensatingEvaporatingFoam solver to model phase change phenomena between liquid and vapor phases of FC-72 within a vessel, incorporating gravity effects and a k-Omega SST turbulence model. The two-phase system neglects surface tension (sigma = 0). Liquid phase properties include density 1583.4 kg/m³, dynamic viscosity 2.64e-7 m²/s, specific heat 1107.2 J/kg/K, and thermal conductivity 0.057 W/m/K. Vapor phase properties are density 14.9 kg/m³, dynamic viscosity 5e-7 m²/s, specific heat 895.2 J/kg/K, and thermal conductivity 0.01 W/m/K, with latent heat 93.0 kJ/kg. Saturation temperature is 367 K. A constant coefficient phase change model is employed with condensation/evaporation coefficients set to 150.

Initial fields are defined as: uniform static pressure 1e5 Pa, modified pressure (p_rgh) 1e5 Pa, velocity zero vector, temperature 366 K, liquid volume fraction 0, turbulence kinetic energy 0.1 m²/s², specific dissipation rate 1 s⁻¹, turbulent viscosity 0 m²/s. Boundary conditions include fixed wall velocities (bottom, left, right) and zero-gradient pressure/temperature; the top boundary uses pressureInletOutletVelocity and inletOutlet conditions; left wall has fixed temperature 360 K (cooled condition); turbulence quantities apply wall functions at solid boundaries.

Discretization employs Euler time scheme, Gauss linear gradients, vanLeer for liquid volume fraction divergence, and Gauss upwind for momentum/energy/turbulence divergence schemes. Laplacian terms use Gauss linear corrected. Pressure equation (p_rgh) uses GAMG with DICGaussSeidel smoother. Velocity equations use smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel. Temperature and liquid volume fraction equations use smoothSolver. Turbulence equations (k, omega) use PBiCGStab with DILU preconditioner. The PIMPLE algorithm uses 1 outer corrector and 2 pressure-velocity correctors without momentum prediction.
