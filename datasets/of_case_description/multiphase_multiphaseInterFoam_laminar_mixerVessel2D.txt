Conduct a 2-dimensional laminar multiphase flow simulation in a mixer vessel using the multiphaseInterFoam solver. The system involves four immiscible Newtonian fluids: water (density 1000 kg/m³, viscosity 1e-06 m²/s), oil (500 kg/m³, 1e-06 m²/s), mercury (13529 kg/m³, 1.125e-07 m²/s), and air (1 kg/m³, 1.48e-05 m²/s). Surface tension coefficients of 0.07 N/m apply between all fluid pairs. The domain includes a rotating zone ("rotor") spinning about the z-axis at 2.2831853 rad/s using the MRF approach, and stationary walls ("stator"), with front/back boundaries treated as empty for 2D simplification. Gravity is disabled.

Initial conditions set all velocity components to zero, modified pressure (p_rgh) to zero, and temperature to 300 K uniformly except at the rotor boundary (fixed at 305 K). Volume fractions for all fluids start at zero throughout the domain. Boundary conditions enforce no-slip velocity at walls, zero-gradient for volume fractions and pressure, and fixed temperature at the rotor. 

Spatial discretization uses Gauss linear schemes for gradients and corrected linear schemes for Laplacians. Divergence terms apply limitedLinearV for momentum, vanLeer for phase fraction advection, and linear for other fluxes. Time integration employs Euler discretization. Solver settings use PCG with DIC preconditioning for pressure (relative tolerance 0.05) and smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel for velocity/temperature. The PIMPLE algorithm executes 4 correction iterations without momentum prediction. Phase fraction equations solve with 4 sub-cycles and interface compression.
