This OpenFOAM simulation employs the interFoam solver to model the interaction between water and air phases within a box undergoing prescribed oscillating motion. The scenario assumes laminar flow dynamics. The box oscillates linearly along the x-axis with an amplitude of 0.1 meters and an angular frequency of 2π rad/s. Gravity acts in the negative y-direction.

Initially, the entire domain contains air (alpha.water = 0). Both velocity and modified pressure (p_rgh) fields are initially zero everywhere. Boundary conditions are configured to handle the moving box walls and the atmosphere interface, utilizing types like movingWallVelocity for walls and inletOutlet/totalPressure for atmosphere surfaces. Surface tension coefficient between phases is set at 0.07 N/m. Water and air properties are defined as Newtonian fluids with their respective densities and kinematic viscosities.

Numerical settings use an Euler scheme for time integration. The vanLeer scheme discretizes the divergence term for the phase fraction (alpha), while upwind is used for momentum and other transported scalars. Gradient terms use Gauss linear discretization. The pressure-velocity coupling is handled by the PIMPLE algorithm with 3 momentum correctors. The pressure equation (p_rgh) is solved using a GAMG solver with DIC smoothing and a relative tolerance of 0.01. Velocity equations use a smoothSolver with GaussSeidel smoothing and zero relative tolerance.
