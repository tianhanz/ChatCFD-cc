This simulation models incompressible, steady-state turbulent flow through a pipe with swirling inlet boundary conditions using the OpenFOAM solver `simpleFoam`. The fluid is Newtonian with a kinematic viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s. The Realizable k-epsilon (realizableKE) turbulence model is employed with wall functions.

The SIMPLE algorithm handles pressure-velocity coupling with default 0 non-orthogonal correctors. The discretization uses Gauss linear schemes for gradients and laplacian terms (with correction). Advection of momentum (`phi,U`) uses a bounded limitedLinearV scheme with a limiting coefficient of 1. Advection for turbulence variables (`k`, `epsilon`, `nuTilda`, `R`) uses a bounded limitedLinear scheme.

Solution settings specify the GAMG linear solver with Gauss-Seidel smoother for pressure (`p`), with tolerance 1e-06 and relative tolerance 0.05. Velocity (`U`), turbulent kinetic energy (`k`), and dissipation rate (`epsilon`) are solved using the smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel smoother, tolerance 1e-05 and relative tolerance 0.1. Relaxation factors are 0.3 for pressure and 0.7 for velocity and turbulence quantities. Residual control targets are set to 0.01 for pressure, and 0.001 for velocity and the turbulence variables (`k|epsilon`).

The initial velocity field is uniform (1, 0, 0) m/s. The initial modified pressure, `k`, and `epsilon` fields are uniform 0 m²/s², 1 m²/s³, and 1 m²/s² respectively. The inlet boundary condition imposes a swirling velocity profile combined with a turbulent intensity of 5% for `k` and a mixing length of 0.5 m for `epsilon`. The outlet uses `inletOutlet` conditions for velocity and turbulence variables, and a fixed zero-pressure condition. All walls apply the no-slip condition for velocity, `kqRWallFunction` for `k`, `epsilonWallFunction` for `epsilon`, and `nutkWallFunction` for turbulent viscosity (`nut`). Cyclic conditions are implied for the pipe flow.
