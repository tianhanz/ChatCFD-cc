Using the interFoam solver to perform a 2D large eddy simulation of turbulent nozzle flow involving two Newtonian fluid phases: fuel and air. The simulation employs a k-equation LES turbulence model. Fuel properties include density of 840 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity of 5.952e-06 m²/s, while air has density 20 kg/m³ and viscosity 8.5e-07 m²/s. Surface tension between phases is set to 0.0261 N/m. Initial conditions show stationary flow with zero fuel volume fraction throughout the domain, modified pressure (p_rgh) at zero, and negligible turbulent kinetic energy (k). Boundary conditions enforce a fixed fuel inlet velocity of 460 m/s in the streamwise direction with pure fuel volume fraction. Wall boundaries impose no-slip velocity conditions. Atmospheric boundary uses total pressure for modified pressure and inletOutlet conditions for volume fraction. Discretization schemes employ Euler time integration, vanLeer divergence for volume fraction advection, linear corrected Laplacians, and corrected face-normal gradients. Solution settings utilize PIMPLE algorithm without momentum prediction, with 2 pressure-velocity correctors and 1 non-orthogonal corrector. Field solvers employ geometric-algebraic multigrid for pressure and Gauss-Seidel smoothing for velocity/turbulence quantities, with specified tolerance and relaxation settings. The turbulence model configuration applies the kEqn subgrid-scale model with smooth delta filtering.
