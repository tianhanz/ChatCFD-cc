This simulation employs the pimpleFoam solver to analyze a 2D incompressible turbulent flow with mesh motion using the k-epsilon turbulence model. The setup features an oscillating inlet motion defined by a solidBody motion solver with a linear oscillation amplitude of 0.5 m in the y-direction at an angular frequency of 3.14 rad/s. The fluid is Newtonian with a kinematic viscosity of 1e-6 m²/s. The domain includes stationary walls, inlet/outlet boundaries, and specialized ACMI coupling interfaces.  

Discretization uses Euler scheme for temporal terms, Gauss linear schemes with gradient limiting for spatial derivatives, and linear upwind schemes for convective terms in turbulence equations. Wall functions (kqRWallFunction, epsilonWallFunction, nutkWallFunction) model near-wall turbulence.  

Solution settings configure the PIMPLE algorithm with 2 pressure correctors and 1 outer corrector. Field solvers include GAMG for pressure (tolerance 1e-5), smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel for velocity/turbulence fields (tolerance 1e-6), and dedicated final solvers with stricter tolerances. Pressure-velocity coupling uses uncorrected flux and disables non-orthogonal corrections.  

Initial conditions set velocity to zero throughout, pressure to zero with outlet fixed at zero value, turbulence kinetic energy (k) to 3.75e-3 m²/s², dissipation rate (epsilon) to 1.8e-3 m²/s³, and eddy viscosity (nut) to zero. Boundary conditions enforce fixed velocity at inlet, pressure-driven outlet flow, no-slip walls with moving mesh adaptation, and coupled interfaces with zero-gradient constraints.
