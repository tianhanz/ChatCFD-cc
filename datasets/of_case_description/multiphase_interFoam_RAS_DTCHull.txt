Employing the interFoam solver to simulate turbulent two-phase flow around a DTCHull ship hull using the k-omega SST turbulence model. The setup involves two immiscible Newtonian fluids: water (density 998.8 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1.09e-06 m²/s) and air (density 1 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1.48e-05 m²/s) without surface tension. Gravity acts in the negative z-direction (0,0,-9.81 m/s²). The initial domain contains only air (alpha.water=0), with uniform inflow velocity of (-1.668,0,0) m/s, turbulence kinetic energy k=0.00015 m²/s², specific dissipation rate omega=2 s⁻¹, and kinematic eddy viscosity nut=5e-07 m²/s.  

Key discretization schemes include: localEuler for time derivatives, Gauss linear gradients with cell limiting for turbulence fields, vanLeer divergence for phase fraction, linearUpwind for momentum and turbulence transport, and corrected surface normal gradients. The PIMPLE algorithm uses 1 outer iteration and 2 pressure-velocity corrections per step, with no non-orthogonal corrections. Momentum prediction is disabled, and equations are solved without relaxation. Solvers are configured with GAMG for pressure and smoothSolver/symGaussSeidel for other variables, using MULES for phase fraction with 2 corrections and 10 limiter iterations. Boundary conditions apply fixed inlet velocity, wall functions on the hull (omegaWallFunction, kqRWallFunction), and variableHeightFlowRate at the outlet for phase fraction.
