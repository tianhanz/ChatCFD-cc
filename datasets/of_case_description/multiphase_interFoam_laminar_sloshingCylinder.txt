The interFoam solver is employed for a laminar simulation of liquid sloshing in an oscillating and rotating cylinder. This multiphase case models the interaction between water and air, both treated as Newtonian fluids. Water has a density of 998.2 kg/m³ and a kinematic viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s, while air has a density of 1 kg/m³ and a kinematic viscosity of 1.48e-05 m²/s. The surface tension coefficient is 0.07 N/m. Gravity is disabled. The cylinder undergoes prescribed motion combining linear oscillation along the x-axis with an amplitude of 0.1 m and angular velocity of 18.894 rad/s, superimposed with rotation about the axis (0, 0.02, 0) with the same angular velocity. Initially, the cylinder is empty of water (alpha.water = 0 throughout the domain), the velocity field is zero everywhere, and the modified pressure field (p_rgh) is uniformly zero. Boundary conditions for velocity use "movingWallVelocity," alpha.water uses "zeroGradient," and p_rgh uses "fixedFluxPressure" on walls. Key discretization settings include an Euler scheme for time derivatives, Gauss linear schemes for gradients and Laplacians, vanLeer divergence schemes for the phase fraction (alpha) and momentum equations, and corrected surface normal gradients. Solvers include smoothSolver (Gauss-Seidel) for velocity, GAMG for pressure, and MULES with 8 limiter iterations for phase fraction. The PIMPLE algorithm uses 2 correctors, 1 non-orthogonal corrector, and disables momentum prediction and phi correction.
