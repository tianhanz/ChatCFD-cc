This simulation employs the interFoam solver to model laminar, two-phase flow involving water and air in a domain with a piston-type wavemaker motion. Water has a kinematic viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s and a density of 1000 kg/m³; air has a kinematic viscosity of 1.48e-05 m²/s and a density of 1 kg/m³. Surface tension is set to 0.07 N/m. The wavemaker acts on the leftwall boundary as a piston, generating waves with a height of 0.06 m and a period of 2.0 s over an initial water depth of 0.25 m. A motion solver handles dynamic mesh deformation, using inverse distance-based diffusion from the leftwall boundary. Initial conditions set the velocity field, modified pressure field (p_rgh), and water volume fraction field (alpha.water) uniformly to zero throughout the domain. The discretization uses the Euler scheme for time derivatives. Gradients use Gauss linear; divergence schemes vary: Gauss linearUpwind for momentum, vanLeer for phase fraction, and interface compression for interface sharpening. Laplacian schemes use Gauss linear corrected. The solution employs the PIMPLE algorithm with 3 pressure-velocity correctors. Momentum prediction is disabled. Solvers for pressure (p_rgh, pcorr) use PCG with DIC preconditioner and relative tolerances ranging from 0 to 0.05. Velocity (U) uses PBiCG with DILU preconditioner.
