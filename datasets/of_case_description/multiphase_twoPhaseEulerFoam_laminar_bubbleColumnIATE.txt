Simulating a laminar bubble column using the twoPhaseEulerFoam solver to model air-water interactions with interfacial transport. The domain features water as the continuous phase and air as the dispersed bubble phase rising under gravity (0, -9.81, 0) m/s². Key physical models include the IATE model for air bubble size evolution with wake coalescence, turbulent breakup, and random coalescence mechanisms, SchillerNaumann drag modeling between phases, constant aspect ratios, and RanzMarshall interfacial heat transfer. Both phases are laminar.

Initial conditions: Uniform air volume fraction (alpha.air) of 0 in the domain, with inlet fixed at 0.5. Uniform air inlet velocity (U.air) is (0, 0.1, 0) m/s while water is stationary. Temperatures are 300K (air) and 350K (water). Pressure starts uniformly at 1e5 Pa. Air bubble interfacial curvature (kappai.air) initializes at 2000 m⁻¹.

Discretization uses Euler scheme for time derivatives, Gauss linear gradients, and vanLeer for air phase divergence. Momentum/energy fluxes apply limitedLinear schemes with coefficient 1. Solution settings employ PIMPLE with 3 outer and 1 inner pressure-velocity iterations. Pressure solves via GAMG with DIC tolerance 1e-8; velocity/interface equations use smoothSolver/symGaussSeidel with tolerance 1e-5. No relaxation is applied.
