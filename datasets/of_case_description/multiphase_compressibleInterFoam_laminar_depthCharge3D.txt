The simulation utilizes the compressibleInterFoam solver for a 3D laminar two-phase flow scenario involving air and water. Gravity acts downwards with a constant acceleration of -9.81 m/s². Surface tension is defined with a coefficient of 0.07 N/m.

Air is modeled as a perfect gas with constant properties: molecular weight 28.9 g/mol, dynamic viscosity 1.84e-05 Pa·s, specific heat 1007 J/kg·K, and Prandtl number 0.7. Water uses a polynomial equation of state and constant properties: molecular weight 18.0 g/mol, dynamic viscosity 3.645e-04 Pa·s, specific heat 4195 J/kg·K, and Prandtl number 2.289. The initial volume fraction of water (`alpha.water`) is uniformly 0 (indicating the domain is initially filled with air). Initial pressure and velocity are uniformly set to 100,000 Pa and (0, 0, 0) m/s respectively, while initial temperature is uniformly 300 K. Boundaries apply no-slip conditions for velocity and fixedFlux pressure conditions.

Discretization employs Euler time integration. Gauss linear schemes handle gradients, while uncorrected schemes manage Laplacian and normal gradients. Advection of the phase fraction uses the vanLeer scheme, while other convective terms (velocity, energy) use Gauss upwind. Solvers for the phase fraction field (`alpha.water`) apply multiple sub-cycles and limiter iterations. Pressure (`p_rgh`) is solved using the GAMG method with DIC smoothing, tolerances of 1e-07 for absolute and 0.01 for relative error. Velocity (U) employs a Gauss-Seidel smoothed solver with a tolerance of 1e-06. The PIMPLE algorithm uses 1 outer correction and 2 pressure-velocity corrections per time step, with momentum prediction disabled.
