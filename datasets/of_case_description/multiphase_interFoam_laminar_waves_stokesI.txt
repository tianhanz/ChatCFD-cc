Using the interFoam solver to conduct a laminar simulation of Stokes I wave propagation in a two-phase water-air system. The model incorporates Newtonian fluid properties for both phases: water has a density of 1000 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s, while air has a density of 1 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity of 1.48e-05 m²/s. Surface tension is modeled with a coefficient of 0.07 N/m. The domain initializes with air only (alpha.water = 0) and quiescent conditions (zero velocity and modified pressure p_rgh throughout).

Wave generation at the inlet uses the Stokes I model with 0.05 m wave height, 3.0 s period, and 3.0 s ramp-up time, including active absorption. The outlet implements shallowWaterAbsorption for wave damping. Boundary conditions enforce wave-specific velocity and pressure treatments at inlet/outlet, fixed walls at the bottom, and atmospheric pressure at the top.

Numerical schemes apply Euler time integration, Gauss linear gradients, and vanLeer divergence for phase fraction convection. Momentum coupling follows PIMPLE with 2 correctors and deactivated momentum predictor. Linear solvers utilize iterative methods: PCG/DIC for pressure and PBiCG/DILU for velocity, with GAMG employed for final pressure refinement. Orthogonal snGrad and linear interpolation complete the spatial discretization.
