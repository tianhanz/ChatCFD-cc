A three-dimensional simulation of a multiphase tank flow is conducted using the OpenFOAM solver driftFluxFoam with the buoyantKEpsilon turbulence model. The case involves two immiscible phases: water (Newtonian fluid) and sludge (Bingham plastic non-Newtonian fluid). Gravity is set to -9.81 m/s² in the y-direction. Initial conditions include zero velocity and uniform sludge volume fraction (α=0) except at the inlet (α=0.002). Turbulence fields k and ε are initialized uniformly at 2.59e-4 m²/s² and 1.973e-7 m²/s³ respectively. Boundary conditions include inlet velocity (0.1315 m/s in x-direction), translating walls, and pressure-driven outlets.

For discretization, the Euler scheme handles time derivatives, while Gauss linear schemes are applied to gradients and corrected Laplacians. The vanLeer scheme resolves divergence of α, and linearUpwind handles momentum convection. Limited linear schemes model turbulence scalar transport.

Solution settings use the PIMPLE algorithm with 3 correction steps (no non-orthogonal corrections) and disabled momentum prediction. The α-field solver employs MULES correction with 3 limiter iterations and smoothSolver. Pressure (p_rgh) utilizes GAMG with Gauss-Seidel smoothing, while velocity/turbulence equations employ PBiCGStab with DILU preconditioning. All equations use a relaxation factor of 1. Physical models define sludge rheology via Bingham plastic parameters and water viscosity (ν=1.78e-06 m²/s). A simple relative velocity model governs interphase slip with drift velocity V₀=(0,-0.002198,0) m/s.
