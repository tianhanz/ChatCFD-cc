The case simulates 2D wave generation by a moving flap in a tank filled with air and water using the interFoam solver for laminar, incompressible, immiscible two-phase flow. The flow is governed by gravity (g = (0 0 -9.81 m/s²)), surface tension (sigma = 0.07 N/m), and kinematic viscosities (water nu = 1e-06 m²/s, air nu = 1.48e-05 m²/s). The water density is 1000 kg/m³ and air density is 1 kg/m³. The left wall flap undergoes prescribed oscillatory motion (waveHeight = 0.06 m, initialDepth = 0.25 m, wavePeriod = 2 s, rampTime = 2 s, flap motionType). A dynamic mesh tracks the flap displacement using a displacementLaplacian motion solver with inverseDistance diffusivity. Initial conditions set velocity to zero everywhere, and the water volume fraction (alpha.water) to zero throughout the domain. Boundary conditions include movingWallVelocity on the leftwall (synchronized with flap motion), waveVelocity on the rightwall (to prevent reflections), pressureInletOutletVelocity on the top, and fixedValue/fixedFluxPressure on solid walls. The simulation employs the PIMPLE algorithm with 3 pressure-velocity correctors and 2 alpha sub-cycles per timestep. Discretization uses the Euler scheme for time derivatives, Gauss linear corrected for Laplacians, vanLeer for alpha convection, and linearUpwind grad(U) for momentum convection. Solvers utilize GAMG/PCG/PBiCG with tolerances ranging from 1e-3 to 1e-10 depending on the variable.
