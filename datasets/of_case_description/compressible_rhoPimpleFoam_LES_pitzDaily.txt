Conduct a large eddy simulation (LES) of compressible flow over the pitzDaily geometry using the `rhoPimpleFoam` solver coupled with the `kEqn` one-equation eddy-viscosity LES turbulence model. The simulation involves compressible gas flow described by perfect gas thermodynamics with a constant dynamic viscosity and specific heat capacity. Key numerical settings employ a backward scheme for time integration. Spatial discretization utilizes Gauss linear for gradients, a corrected scheme for Laplacians, and specific divergence schemes: LUST grad for momentum `div(phi,U)` and internal energy `div(phi,e)`, while limitedLinear is applied for turbulence quantities `div(phi,k)` and `div(phi,nuTilda)`. The pressure-velocity coupling employs the PIMPLE algorithm with 3 outer correctors and momentum prediction enabled. The Preconditioned Conjugate Gradient (PCG) method with DIC preconditioning solves the pressure equation, and the smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel smoothing handles velocity and turbulence variable systems, with relative tolerances set to 0.01 during iterations. Boundary conditions enforce turbulent inlet velocity with specified fluctuations for `U`, fixed inlet temperature `T=300 K`, fixed inlet turbulence kinetic energy `k`, fixed `nuTilda=0`, zero-gradient pressure at the inlet, and pressureInletOutletVelocity at the outlet. Walls use no-slip velocity and fixed temperature conditions. Initial conditions set uniform pressure `1e5 Pa`, uniform temperature `300 K`, zero velocity, and zero values for turbulence fields (`k`, `nut`, `alphat`, `nuTilda`).
