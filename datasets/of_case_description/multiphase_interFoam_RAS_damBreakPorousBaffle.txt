Using the interFoam solver to conduct a 2D/3D dam-break flow simulation involving a porous baffle structure, incorporating the k-epsilon turbulence model. The system comprises two Newtonian fluid phases: water (density 1000 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1e-06 m²/s) and air (density 1 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1.48e-05 m²/s), with a surface tension coefficient of 0.07 N/m. A gravity vector of (0, -9.81, 0) m/s² is applied. The porous baffle is modelled via pressure boundaries ('porous_half0', 'porous_half1') with Darcy-Forchheimer coefficients D=1000 and I=500, thickness 0.15 m, using a pressure jump condition to represent flow resistance.

Initial conditions include a zero velocity field ('U'), zero modified pressure ('p_rgh'), and a water volume fraction ('alpha.water') initialized uniformly as 0 (indicating air-filled domain initially). Turbulence fields ('k', 'epsilon') start uniformly at 0.1 m²/s² and m²/s³ respectively, while turbulent viscosity ('nut') is initialized as 0. The atmosphere boundary uses an inletOutlet condition for alpha.water and velocity, and a totalPressure condition for p_rgh. Walls employ noSlip velocity, zeroGradient alpha.water, and wall functions for turbulence fields. The porous baffle patches are treated as cyclic with specialized porousBafflePressure boundaries.

Discretization employs Euler time scheme. Convective terms use vanLeer for volume fraction, linearUpwind for velocity, and upwind for k/epsilon. Pressure is solved with the PCG method and DIC preconditioner. Volume fraction uses MULES with 3 limiter iterations and a smoothSolver. Velocity and turbulence fields are solved with the smoothSolver and symGaussSeidel smoother. The PIMPLE algorithm uses 3 outer correctors without momentum prediction.
