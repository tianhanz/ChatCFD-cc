Employ the pimpleFoam solver to simulate an incompressible turbulent airflow induced by a rotating fan within an enclosed room. The simulation uses the k-omega SST turbulence model. The fan blade rotates around a defined axis at a constant angular velocity. The fluid is Newtonian with a kinematic viscosity of 1e-05 m²/s and gravity acts vertically downward.

The PIMPLE algorithm is configured for transient flow, performing momentum prediction and employing 3 outer correctors per time step with 1 momentum corrector. Pressure is solved using a GAMG linear solver with a DICGaussSeidel smoother, while velocity and turbulence variables (k, omega) use a smoothSolver with a symmetric GaussSeidel smoother. Target solver tolerances are set to 1e-06.

Spatial discretization uses an Euler scheme for time derivatives. The Gauss linear scheme computes gradients. Convective fluxes for velocity and turbulent quantities (k, omega) employ the linearUpwind scheme, and diffusion terms use the Gauss linear corrected scheme for laplacians. Corrected schemes are used for surface normal gradients. The mesh motion solver is active for the rotating fan zone. Boundary conditions include cyclic patches for mesh interfaces, a moving wall for the fan blades, specified inlet velocity at the door, and outlet pressure conditions. Wall functions model turbulence near solid walls. Initial fields are uniform: velocity zero, pressure zero, k=0.00341 m²/s², omega=0.1 1/s, nut=1e-05 m²/s.
