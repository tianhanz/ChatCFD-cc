Employ the interFoam solver to conduct a 2-dimensional laminar simulation of a solitary wave in a two-phase water-air system. Both fluids are Newtonian. Water has a density of 1000 kg/m³ and a kinematic viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s, while air has a density of 1 kg/m³ and a kinematic viscosity of 1.48e-05 m²/s. The surface tension coefficient is 0.07 N/m, and gravity acts downwards in the z-direction at -9.81 m/s².

At the inlet, a Boussinesq wave model is applied with a wave height of 0.3 m and active wave absorption enabled. The outlet uses a shallow water absorption wave model. Boundary conditions for the water phase fraction (alpha.water) and velocity (U) utilize specialized wave types (`waveAlpha`, `waveVelocity`) at the inlet and outlet, a fixed zero value velocity on the ground, and an inletOutlet condition on the top.

The simulation utilizes an Euler scheme for time derivatives. Spatial gradients are discretized using Gauss linear schemes. For the divergence terms, the velocity uses a Gauss linearUpwind scheme with `grad(U)`, the phase fraction employs the Gauss vanLeer scheme, and the buoyancy-modified stress term uses Gauss linear. Laplacian and interpolation schemes default to linear orthogonal methods. SnGrad schemes are orthogonal.

Solution is achieved using a PIMPLE algorithm (no momentum predictor, 2 correctors, no non-orthogonal correctors). Solvers are set for the fields: PBiCG with DILU preconditioner for velocity, PCG with DIC preconditioner for pressure correction, and PCG/DIC for p_rgh during iteration (tolerance 1e-6, relTol 0.1), switching to GAMG/DIC for the final p_rgh solution (tolerance 1e-7). One alpha corrector with 3 sub-cycles is used.
