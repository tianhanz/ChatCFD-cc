This case simulates laminar natural convection of a Boussinesq fluid in an enclosure, driven by a temperature difference between the bottom (301 K) and top (300 K) walls, with insulated side walls. Gravity acts vertically downward. The fluid properties are Newtonian with kinematic viscosity 1e-03 m²/s, thermal expansion coefficient 1e-03 K⁻¹, and laminar Prandtl number 1.0. Initial fields are uniform: velocity has a small x-component (1e-04 m/s), temperature is 300 K, modified pressure (p_rgh) is 0, and turbulence fields (k, epsilon, nut, alphat) are set to 0 or negligible values.

Discretization utilizes Euler for time derivatives. Velocity divergence uses a Gauss linearUpwind scheme, while temperature divergence uses Gauss limitedLinear. Pressure gradients and laplacians use Gauss linear and Gauss linear corrected, respectively.

Solution employs the buoyantBoussinesqPimpleFoam solver with the PIMPLE algorithm (momentum prediction disabled, 2 correctors per step). The pressure (p_rgh) is solved with GAMG using DIC smoother and relative tolerance 0.01. Velocity and temperature equations are solved with PBiCGStab using DILU preconditioner and relative tolerance 0.01. No relaxation factors are applied.
