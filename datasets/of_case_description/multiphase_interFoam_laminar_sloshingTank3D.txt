The OpenFOAM simulation models three-dimensional sloshing dynamics within an oscillating tank using the interFoam solver under laminar flow conditions. Two Newtonian fluid phases are present: water with a density of 998.2 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s, and air with a density of 1 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity of 1.48e-05 m²/s; surface tension is neglected (sigma = 0). The tank motion follows a ship dynamics approximation (SDA) with specified oscillation amplitudes (heave, sway, roll), centered at the origin (CofG (0 0 0)) and influenced by gravity (g = (0 0 -9.81) m/s²). The volume of fluid (alpha.water) is initialized uniformly as zero (dry state), velocity (U) and modified pressure (p_rgh) start at zero. Pressure-velocity coupling employs the PIMPLE algorithm with 2 non-orthogonal correctors, disabling momentum prediction and phi correction; pressure reference is set to 1e5 Pa at point (0 0 0.15) m. Discretization uses Euler time stepping, Gauss linear gradients, and vanLeer schemes for divergence terms (rhoPhi,U and phi,alpha) to capture the sharp interface. Solvers include GAMG/PCG for pressure (p_rgh) with DIC/DICGaussSeidel preconditioning and smoothSolver (GaussSeidel) for velocity; alpha solves employ 1 correction and 3 sub-cycles with interface compression (cAlpha = 1.5). No relaxation is applied to velocity equations.
