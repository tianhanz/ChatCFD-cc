We are simulating an irregular multi-directional wave field using the interFoam solver for incompressible two-phase flow, employing a laminar approach without turbulence modeling. The simulation captures air-water interactions with free surface dynamics, initialized as an air-filled domain. Key physical models include gravity (g = (0, 0, -9.81) m/s²), surface tension (σ = 0.07 N/m), and Newtonian fluid properties: water (ν = 1e-06 m²/s, ρ = 1000 kg/m³) and air (ν = 1.48e-05 m²/s, ρ = 1 kg/m³).

Wave generation at the inlet utilizes an irregularMultiDirectional model with 57 spectral components, including explicit wave periods, heights, phases, and directions. Active absorption suppresses reflections at the inlet, with a 18-second ramp time to stabilize wave initialization. The outlet implements shallowWaterAbsorption.

Discretization settings use Euler time integration and van Leer scheme for phase fraction advection. Pressure-velocity coupling employs PIMPLE with 2 corrections and momentum prediction disabled. Solvers include PBiCG-DILU for velocity (tolerance 1e-6) and PCG-DIC for pressure (tolerance 1e-6, relTol 0.1), with dedicated final pressure/velocity solvers for strict convergence. Boundary conditions enforce wave-based α.water and U fields at inlet/outlet, slip walls at sides, no-slip at the ground, and pressure-driven outflow at the top.
