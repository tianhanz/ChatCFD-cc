This simulation models compressible steady-state flow through an angled duct containing a porous region using the rhoSimpleFoam solver. The turbulence is modeled with the standard k-epsilon RANS model. The fluid is air, treated as a perfect gas with constant specific heat (Cp = 1005 J/kg·K), molecular weight (28.9 g/mol), and Sutherland law viscosity. Within the "porosity" cell zone, explicit fixed-coefficient porosity is applied to model flow resistance using linear coefficients in a rotated coordinate system. Additionally, a fixed temperature of 350 K and fixed turbulence values (k = 1 m²/s², epsilon = 150 m²/s³) are enforced throughout this porous region. Key boundary conditions include a mass flow inlet (0.1 kg/s) with turbulent kinetic energy specified via 5% intensity and dissipation rate via mixing length (0.005 m), fixed static pressure outlet, no-slip walls for solid boundaries, and a slip condition on the porous media interface. Discretization employs first-order upwind for convection terms (momentum, energy, turbulence), linear corrected scheme for Laplacians, and Gauss linear for gradients. Solution settings use GAMG for pressure with tight tolerance (1e-08), smooth solvers for velocity and energy, and under-relaxation factors (pressure: 0.3, velocity: 0.7, turbulence: 0.7, energy: 0.5). Convergence is monitored for residuals (pressure: 0.01, velocity: 1e-4, energy: 0.001, turbulence: 0.001).
