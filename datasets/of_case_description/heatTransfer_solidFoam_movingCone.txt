The movingCone case employs the solidFoam solver for thermo-mechanical analysis of a solid cone experiencing prescribed motion. The cone is modelled as a linear elastic isotropic solid with constant thermophysical properties: density 8000 kg/m³, specific heat 450 J/kg/K, and thermal conductivity 80 W/m/K. Radiation effects are disabled.  

Mesh deformation is governed by a velocity-based motion solver acting primarily in the x-direction ("velocityComponentLaplacian"), enforcing a specified displacement field. Key boundary conditions include:
*   **Motion (pointMotionUx):** A constant velocity (1 unit) prescribed on the moving wall ("movingWall"), zero velocity on "fixedWall" and "left" boundaries, and slip conditions elsewhere.
*   **Temperature (T):** Fixed value (600 K) at the "left" boundary; zero-gradient (adiabatic) on all walls and "farField".
*   **Pressure (p) & Velocity (U):** Derived fields primarily using zero-gradient pressure boundaries, no-slip conditions for velocity on relevant walls, and a pressureInletOutlet velocity condition at "left".

Numerical schemes utilize:
*   **Temporal:** Euler (ddt default).
*   **Spatial:** Gauss linear gradients (grad), uncorrected Laplacian for motion, corrected Laplacian otherwise, corrected face normal gradients (snGrad).
*   **Solvers:** Preconditioned Conjugate Gradient (PCG) with Diagonal Incomplete Cholesky (DIC) for thermal field (h) and motion field (cellMotionUx), with relative tolerance 0.1 for h and absolute tolerance 1e-08 for motion. PIMPLE algorithm employs 2 outer correctors and 1 momentum corrector with no mesh flux or non-orthogonal corrections. No equation relaxation is applied.  

The simulation requirement involves analyzing the coupled thermal and elastic response of the moving solid cone structure under the described loading and constraints.
