This simulation models buoyant turbulent flow with thermal radiation in an enclosed room containing a heated box using OpenFOAM. The buoyantSimpleFoam solver is employed for steady-state analysis of compressible flow with natural convection. The k-epsilon RANS model captures turbulence effects. Thermal radiation is implemented via the P1 model with uniform absorption/emission coefficients of 0.5.

Spatial discretization utilizes linear Gauss schemes for gradients and laplacians. Convection terms for momentum (U), energy (h), and turbulence (k/epsilon) apply bounded upwind differencing. Velocity-pressure coupling follows the SIMPLE algorithm with pressure solved via PCG/DIC solvers and velocity/energy via PBiCGStab/DILU. Field relaxation is applied (p_rgh: 0.7, U/h: 0.2, k/epsilon: 0.5).

Boundaries include four walls: floor (300K), ceiling (300K), fixedWalls (adiabatic), and a hot box (500K). All walls impose no-slip velocity conditions and wall functions for turbulence (nut, epsilon, k). Radiation boundaries use Marshak conditions with blackbody assumptions (emissivity=absorptivity=1.0). Pressure boundaries are fixedFluxPressure type. The working fluid is air with constant properties: dynamic viscosity 1.8e-05 Pa·s, Prandtl number 0.7, specific heat 1000 J/kg·K, molecular weight 28.9 g/mol. Gravity is (0, 0, -9.81) m/s². Initial fields are quiescent velocity and uniform pressure (100 kPa), temperature (300K), and turbulence fields (k=0.1 m²/s², ε=0.01 m²/s³).
