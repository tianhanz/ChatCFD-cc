This simulation employs the icoFoam solver to model a two-dimensional laminar, incompressible flow through a 90-degree pipe elbow geometry. The working fluid is set with a constant kinematic viscosity of 0.01 m²/s. Boundary conditions define two velocity inlets: one specified with a uniform velocity of (1 0 0) m/s and another with (0 3 0) m/s, both utilizing fixedValue. Walls enforce the no-slip condition. The pressure outlet is a fixedValue of 0, while all other boundaries use zeroGradient for pressure.

Discretization utilizes an Euler scheme for time derivatives. Spatial gradients are handled with Gauss linear interpolation. The convection term (div(phi,U)) employs a Gauss limitedLinearV scheme with a flux limiter value of 1. Laplacian terms and surface normal gradients use corrected schemes.

Pressure is solved using the Preconditioned Conjugate Gradient (PCG) method with Diagonal Incomplete Cholesky (DIC) preconditioning, a relative tolerance of 0.05, and an absolute tolerance of 1e-06. Velocity is solved using a smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel smoothing and an absolute tolerance of 1e-05. The transient solution is advanced using the PISO algorithm with 2 pressure correctors and 2 non-orthogonal correctors per time step.
