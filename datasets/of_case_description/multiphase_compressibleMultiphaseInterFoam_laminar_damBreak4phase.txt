This simulation models a four-phase dam-break scenario involving water, oil, mercury, and air using the compressibleMultiphaseInterFoam solver. The flow is laminar, and gravitational acceleration is defined as (0, -9.81, 0) m/s². Initial conditions set all velocity fields to zero, with air occupying most of the domain and the other three fluids contained behind the dam structure. Pressure fields are initialized uniformly at 100,000 Pa. Phase fractions use a vanLeer divergence scheme to maintain boundedness. Velocity and pressure coupling employs the PIMPLE algorithm with 2 momentum correctors. Pressure equations are solved using a GAMG solver with Gauss-Seidel smoothing and a relative tolerance of 0.05. Velocity equations utilize a smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel smoothing and a relative tolerance of 0.1. Surface tension coefficients between all fluid pairs are uniformly set to 0.07 N/m. Material properties are constant for each phase (e.g., water density 1027 kg/m³, mercury density 13529 kg/m³, air dynamic viscosity 1.84e-05 Pa·s). Wall boundaries enforce no-slip conditions for velocity, fixed static contact angles of 90° for all phase pairs, and a fixed pressure boundary for the atmosphere.
