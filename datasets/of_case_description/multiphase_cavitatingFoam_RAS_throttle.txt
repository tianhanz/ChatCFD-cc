The cavitatingFoam solver is employed to simulate cavitation in a throttle valve using the kOmegaSST turbulence model for incompressible, turbulent flow. The domain is initially filled with liquid water at rest. Water (rho: 830 kg/m³, nu: 7.831e-06 m²/s) and vapor (rho: 0.14 kg/m³, nu: 4.252e-05 m²/s) are modeled as Newtonian fluids. Saturation properties include pSat = 4500 Pa and linear barotropic compressibility. Initial pressure is 3e6 Pa at inlet and 1e6 Pa at outlet, inducing cavitation. Density is initialized uniformly at 845 kg/m³. Turbulence parameters k and omega start at 10 m²/s² and 77200 s⁻¹ respectively. Boundary conditions include totalPressure inlet, fixedValue outlet pressure, and noSlip walls.  

Discretization uses Euler time stepping, Gauss linear gradients, corrected laplacians, and limitedLinear divergence schemes for rho, U, k, omega. The PIMPLE algorithm performs 3 pressure-velocity corrections per time step with no relaxation. Solvers: smoothSolver (symGaussSeidel) for rho/U/k/omega with tolerance 1e-8, relTol 0.1; GAMG (GaussSeidel) for pressure with relTol 0.1. Turbulence wall functions apply at boundaries.
