We are simulating a buoyancy-driven turbulent flow with heat transfer in a 3D room configuration using the OpenFOAM solver buoyantPimpleFoam. This example, named hotRoom, models convective heat transfer where air (treated as a perfect gas) is heated, generating buoyancy effects. Gravity acts downward with (0, -9.81, 0) m/s². The RANS k-epsilon model is used for turbulence closure with wall functions applied to boundaries (floor, ceiling, walls). Initial conditions include uniform static pressure (1e5 Pa), zero velocity, uniform temperature (300 K), turbulent kinetic energy (0.1 m²/s²), and dissipation rate (0.01 m²/s³). Key boundary conditions: the floor has fixed-value temperature (300 K), the ceiling uses lumped-mass thermal modeling with 1000 kg mass and 4100 J/kgK specific heat, and walls are adiabatic. The fluid properties are constant: molecular weight 28.9 g/mol, Cp=1000 J/kgK, dynamic viscosity 1.8e-5 Pa·s, and Pr=0.7.

Discretization schemes: Euler implicit time integration, linear Gauss gradients, upwind convection for momentum/energy/turbulence, corrected linear surface normal gradients. Solvers: PCG with DIC preconditioner for pressure (tolerance 1e-8), PBiCGStab with DILU for velocity/turbulence/energy (tolerance 1e-6). PIMPLE algorithm uses momentum predictor, 1 outer correction, and 2 pressure corrections with reference pressure 1e5 Pa at cell 0. No mesh non-orthogonality handling is applied.
