The OpenFOAM case "RAS/floatingObject" employs the interFoam solver to simulate the interaction of two immiscible, incompressible fluids (water and air) with a dynamically moving rigid body object. A Reynolds-Averaged Navier-Stokes (RANS) turbulence model is utilized, specifically the k-epsilon model. The cuboid-shaped floating object is constrained to rotate about the Y-axis and translate vertically within the domain. It has defined dimensions (0.3m x 0.2m x 0.5m), density (500 kg/m³), and calculated mass and moment of inertia. Gravity (0, 0, -9.81 m/s²) acts on the system.

For discretization, the time derivative uses the second-order accurate Crank-Nicolson scheme. Gradient terms are discretized with linear Gauss schemes. Divergence schemes include vanLeer for the interface-capturing (alpha) and convective fluxes (rhoPhi,U), while upwind schemes handle turbulent kinetic energy (k) and dissipation rate (epsilon) convection. Laplacian and surface normal gradient terms use linear corrected Gauss schemes.

Solution settings employ a PIMPLE algorithm with 3 outer correctors, 1 pressure corrector, momentum prediction disabled, and mesh motion enabled per outer iteration. Solvers include smoothSolver with Gauss-Seidel smoother for the volume fraction (alpha.water), turbulent fields (k, epsilon), and velocity (U); GAMG with DIC smoother for modified pressure (p_rgh); and PCG with GAMG preconditioning for pressure correction (pcorr). Wall functions model turbulence behavior near the floating object and stationary walls. The floating object's boundary condition uses movingWallVelocity, while stationary walls apply noSlip. Modified pressure (p_rgh) boundaries use fixedFluxPressure for walls and totalPressure for the atmosphere.
