Employing the interIsoFoam solver to simulate a three-dimensional dam-break flow interacting with an obstacle under laminar flow conditions. The simulation involves two Newtonian fluid phases: water with a kinematic viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s and density of 1000 kg/m³, and air with a kinematic viscosity of 1.48e-05 m²/s and density of 1 kg/m³. The surface tension coefficient is 0.07 N/m, and gravity acts downwards with a value of 9.81 m/s² in the z-direction. An adaptive mesh refinement technique is dynamically applied based on the water volume fraction field (alpha.water), targeting refinement levels between 0.001 and 0.999, with a maximum refinement level of 2. The volume-of-fluid (VOF) interface capturing employs the PLIC reconstruction scheme (plicRDF). A pressure-velocity coupling approach using the PIMPLE algorithm is utilized, configured for 3 pressure corrections per iteration without momentum prediction and 1 non-orthogonal corrector. Pressure (p_rgh) is solved using a GAMG solver with algebraic multigrid, while velocity (U) employs a smoothSolver with Gauss-Seidel smoothing. Initial conditions set the entire domain to air (alpha.water = 0), zero velocity throughout, and zero modified pressure (p_rgh). Boundary conditions define walls and the obstacle with zero velocity and fixedFluxPressure for pressure, with the atmosphere boundary using inletOutlet for phase fraction and totalPressure for pressure. Discretization schemes default to Euler for time derivatives, Gauss linear for gradients, and Gauss linear corrected for Laplacian terms; divergence terms for momentum and phase transport use Gauss upwind. The mesh adapts every time step based on the water fraction field.
