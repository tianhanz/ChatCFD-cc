Using the interFoam solver to conduct a 2D simulation of Stokes II wave propagation in a laminar flow regime involving two Newtonian phases (water and air). The physical setup includes gravity (9.81 m/s² downward), surface tension (coefficient 0.07 N/m), and prescribed wave boundary conditions. Water properties are density 1000 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity 1e-6 m²/s; air properties are density 1 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity 1.48e-5 m²/s. The waves at the inlet have a height of 0.05 m, period of 3.0 s, and a 3.0 s ramp-up time, with active absorption enabled. The outlet uses shallow water absorption.

Discretization employs the Euler scheme for time derivatives, Gauss linear scheme for gradients, orthogonal scheme for Laplacians, and linear interpolation. The divergence scheme uses Gauss vanLeer for phase fraction (alpha), linearUpwind for momentum (rhoPhi,U), and linear for interfacial compression term (phirb,alpha). 

Initial fields are uniform: zero velocity throughout the domain, modified pressure (p_rgh) at zero, and initial air volume fraction uniform at 1 (water alpha=0). The PIMPLE algorithm applies 2 correctors with no momentum prediction and no non-orthogonal correctors. Solvers include PBiCG/DILU for velocity with tolerance 1e-6 (relTol 0.1), PCG/DIC for pressure predictor (tolerance 1e-6, relTol 0.1), and GAMG for final pressure solution (tolerance 1e-7). Phase fraction solution uses 3 sub-cycles per time step.
