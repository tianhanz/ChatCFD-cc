This simulation employs the interIsoFoam solver to model a laminar, immiscible two-phase flow (water and air) with gravity acting in the negative y-direction at 9.81 m/s². The fluid phases are Newtonian, with water having a density of 1000 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s, while air has a density of 1 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity of 1.48e-05 m²/s. Surface tension effects are neglected (sigma = 0).  
Initially, the domain is filled entirely with air (alpha.water = 0 everywhere), and both velocity and modified pressure (p_rgh) fields are uniformly zero. The simulation uses the Euler scheme for time derivatives and Gauss linear discretization for gradients. The interface capturing employs the plicRDF reconstruction scheme with frozen flow enabled (momentum equations not solved), and pressure corrections are deactivated. Boundary conditions include fixedValue (alpha.water = 0) and zeroGradient for velocity/pressure at the rim, with empty patches implying a 2D configuration.
