This simulation models laminar buoyant flow with coupled heat transfer and radiation using the buoyantPimpleFoam solver. The case focuses on thermocouple interactions in an enclosure initially filled with stationary air at 298 K and 1 bar pressure. All solid boundaries ("walls") are fixed at 873 K, driving natural convection and radiative heat transfer.  

Radiation is modeled using the fvDOM (Finite Volume Discrete Ordinates Method) with 3 azimuthal and 5 polar directions. Absorption and emission coefficients are constant (0.01 m⁻¹ each). Grey-diffusive boundary radiation treats walls with emissivity/absorptivity/transmissivity of 1.0/1.0/0.0. Radiation solves every 10 flow iterations.

Discretization uses Euler for time, Gauss linear for gradients, and upwind for convective terms (momentum, energy). Pressure-velocity coupling employs PIMPLE with 1 outer loop and 2 correctors. Solver settings include PCG/DIC for pressure, PBiCGStab/DILU for velocity and energy, and GAMG/symGaussSeidel for radiation intensity (Ii). Initial fields: Uniform static pressure (1e5 Pa), modified pressure (p_rgh = 1e5 Pa), zero velocity, uniform temperature 298 K (walls: 873 K), and zero radiation intensity.
