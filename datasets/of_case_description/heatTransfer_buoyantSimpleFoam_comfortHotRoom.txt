Simulating buoyant flow in a room for thermal comfort analysis using buoyantSimpleFoam with a steady-state approach. The case models incompressible airflow with Boussinesq buoyancy approximation, where gravity is (0, 0, -9.81) m/s². Turbulence is modeled with the k-epsilon RANS approach. 

Discretization uses steady-state time derivatives. Gradients employ Gauss linear scheme. Convective terms use bounded Gauss upwind for velocity, enthalpy, turbulent kinetic energy, dissipation rate, and age fields, with Gauss linear for specific stress terms. Laplacian and surface normal gradients use orthogonal schemes. 

Solver settings: Pressure (p_rgh) uses GAMG with GaussSeidel smoother (tol: 1e-8, relTol: 0.01). Velocity, enthalpy, and turbulence variables (k/epsilon) use PBiCGStab with DILU preconditioner (tol: 1e-7, relTol: 0.1). SIMPLE algorithm disables momentum prediction and non-orthogonal correctors, enforcing residual control (1e-6) for p_rgh, velocity, enthalpy, and turbulence. Relaxation factors are 0.7 for pressure, 0.3 for velocity/enhalpy equations, and 0.7 for turbulence terms.

Constraints include temperature limiting (280-310 K) and velocity damping (max 10 m/s) applied everywhere. Initial fields: Uniform velocity (0,0,0), temperature 302 K, pressure 1e5 Pa, turbulence quantities (k=0.08 m²/s², ε=0.23 m²/s³), and thermal diffusivity 0. Boundaries: Walls have no-slip velocity, fixed temperature, and turbulence wall functions; inlet has fixed velocity (0.2 m/s x-direction), temperature 290 K, and turbulent inlet conditions; outlet uses pressure-driven velocity and zero-gradient temperature.
