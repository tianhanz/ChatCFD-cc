Simulate a two-dimensional laminar incompressible flow with immiscible fluids using interIsoFoam, focusing on interface capturing of a disc-shaped region in constant flow with periodic boundaries. The domain uses cyclic conditions horizontally and empty boundaries vertically, indicating planar symmetry. Fluid phases include water (density 1000 kg/m³, viscosity 1e-06 m²/s) and air (density 1 kg/m³, viscosity 1.48e-05 m²/s), both Newtonian, with surface tension disabled. Gravity acts vertically downward at 9.81 m/s².

Initial conditions set uniform air phase (alpha.water = 0), velocity (-0.2, 0, -0.05) m/s, and zero modified pressure (p_rgh). The solver configures frozen flow, solving only interface advection via isoAlpha geometric reconstruction with clipping and no sub-cycling. Momentum prediction is disabled, and velocity remains prescribed without solving momentum equations. Discretization employs Euler time scheme, Gauss linear gradients, linearUpwind for divergence terms with velocity gradient dependence, and corrected schemes for Laplacian/surface-normal gradients. Linear interpolation applies throughout. Solvers use conjugate gradient methods with algebraic preconditioning: PCG-DIC for pressure (relative tolerance 0.05) and PBiCG-DILU for velocity (absolute tolerance 1e-06), with stricter pressure-correction tolerance (1e-10). Non-orthogonal correction is disabled.
