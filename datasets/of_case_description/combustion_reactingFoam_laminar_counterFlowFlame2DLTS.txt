This simulation models a two-dimensional laminar counterflow methane-air diffusion flame using the reactingFoam solver with local time stepping (LTS). The laminar flow configuration involves opposed fuel and air streams meeting at the centerline. The left (fuel) inlet injects pure methane at 293 K with velocity (0.1, 0, 0) m/s, while the right (air) inlet introduces air (23% O2, 77% N2 by mass fraction) at 293 K with velocity (-0.1, 0, 0) m/s. The outlet uses a pressure boundary condition. Initial fields set pressure uniformly to 1e5 Pa, temperature uniformly to 2000 K, and velocities/internal species mass fractions to zero or defined values. Transport uses Sutherland viscosity with thermochemistry defined by JANAF polynomial coefficients and perfect gas law. Combustion is modeled using detailed methane-air chemistry kinetics.  

Numerical discretization employs the local Euler scheme for time derivatives. Gradients are discretized with Gauss linear schemes. Divergence terms for convection of velocity, species, and turbulence scalars use limitedLinear differencing (limited to preserve boundedness). Laplacian and interpolation schemes default to Gauss linear orthogonal and linear, respectively.  

Solution settings use the PIMPLE algorithm with momentum prediction disabled and only one outer/corrector loop. Pressure is solved with the PCG method and DIC preconditioner. Velocities and scalars (temperature, species mass fractions, turbulence scalars) use the PBiCGStab method with DILU preconditioner. Solver tolerances for all equations are 1e-6 absolute and 0.1 relative.
