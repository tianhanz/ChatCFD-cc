This simulation employs the interIsoFoam solver to model a sphere interacting with a reversed vortex flow scenario. The problem involves two immiscible Newtonian fluids: water and air. Water has a density of 1000 kg/m³ and a kinematic viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s, while air has a density of 1 kg/m³ and a kinematic viscosity of 1.48e-05 m²/s. Surface tension is neglected (sigma = 0 N/m). Gravity acts in the negative y-direction with a magnitude of 9.81 m/s². The flow is simulated as laminar.

The initial flow field is stationary everywhere, with uniform zero velocity (`U`) and uniform zero modified pressure (`p_rgh`). The initial volume fraction of water (`alpha.water`) is zero throughout the domain, indicating the domain is filled with air initially.

For spatial discretization, gradients are computed using the `Gauss linear` scheme. The divergence term for `rhoPhi*U` uses the `Gauss limitedLinearV 1` scheme. Laplacian terms use `Gauss linear corrected`. Interpolation uses the `linear` scheme, and surface normal gradients use the `corrected` scheme. The time derivative scheme is set to `Euler`.

Within the pressure-velocity coupling (PIMPLE), `frozenFlow` is activated and `momentumPredictor` is disabled. The interface between the fluids is reconstructed using the `isoAlpha` scheme (`reconstructionScheme`) with specific tolerances (`isoFaceTol`, `surfCellTol` set to 1e-8), no clipping (`clip false`), and one sub-cycle (`nAlphaSubCycles 1`).
