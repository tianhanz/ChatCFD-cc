Simulate two-dimensional laminar planar Poiseuille flow of a Newtonian fluid using the `pimpleFoam` solver. The fluid has a kinematic viscosity (`nu`) of 0.1 m²/s. A constant momentum source applies a uniform specific acceleration of (5 0 0) m/s² to the entire domain from the start to drive the flow, representing a constant pressure gradient.

Initial conditions set velocity (`U`), relative pressure (`p`), and viscoelastic stress (`sigma`) fields uniformly to zero throughout the domain. Wall boundaries enforce a no-slip condition (fixed value `(0 0 0)` for `U`) and zero normal pressure gradient (zeroGradient for `p` and `sigma`).

Key discretization schemes include:
-   Time derivative: **Backward**.
-   Convection term for velocity: **Gauss linearUpwind grad(U)**.
-   Convection term for stress: **Gauss vanAlbada**.
-   Pressure gradient: **Gauss linear**.
-   Laplacian terms: **Gauss linear uncorrected**.

Solution settings (`fvSolution`) specify:
-   Pressure (`p`) solved with **GAMG** solver (tolerance 1e-6, relTol 0.05), smoothed with **DIC**.
-   Velocity and stress (`U` or `sigma`) solved with **smoothSolver** using **symGaussSeidel** smoother (tolerance 1e-6, relTol 0.1).
-   PIMPLE algorithm configured for **15 outer correctors** and **3 pressure correctors** per outer iteration, with **momentum prediction turned off**.
