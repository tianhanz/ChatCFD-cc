This OpenFOAM simulation utilizes the pimpleFoam solver to model an incompressible flow around a surface-mounted cube using Large Eddy Simulation (LES). The SpalartAllmarasIDDES turbulence model is employed, relying on the nuTilda transport variable. The fluid is Newtonian with a constant kinematic viscosity of 2.5e-05 m²/s.

The pressure field is solved using the GAMG solver with a DICGaussSeidel smoother and a relative tolerance of 0.01. Velocity (U), turbulent kinetic energy (k), and nuTilda are solved using the PBiCGStab solver with a DILU preconditioner and a relative tolerance of 0.1. Tighter tolerance settings ("Final") exist for convergence. The PIMPLE algorithm uses 3 outer correctors and 1 inner corrector, with no non-orthogonal correction.

Discretization uses backward differencing for temporal gradients. The divergence scheme for the momentum equation (div(phi,U)) is the DEShybrid scheme, combining linear and linearUpwind schemes with a DES coefficient of 0.65. Other divergence terms (k, nuTilda, phi,B) use the limitedLinear scheme with a coefficient of 0.1. Least squares and linear schemes are used for gradients and interpolation respectively.

Initial conditions include uniform velocity zero, pressure zero, k of 1e-05 m²/s², and nuTilda of 7.5e-05 m²/s. Boundary conditions feature a turbulentDFSEMInlet at the inlet with mapped turbulence profiles for U, R, and L. Outflow uses an inletOutlet condition for U with zero inlet value and fixedValue zero for pressure. Sides have a slip condition. The cube and top/bottom surfaces have no-slip conditions for velocity, zero gradient for pressure, and nutUSpaldingWallFunction for nut; k and nuTilda are fixed at zero on these walls.
