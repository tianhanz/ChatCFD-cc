Employ the interFoam solver to simulate laminar two-phase flow involving water and air in a test tube mixer with dynamic mesh motion. The tube undergoes compound rotational motion: one rotation around the z-axis at 6.2832 rad/s centered at (0, 0.1, 0), and a rocking motion around the origin at 40 rad/s with 45-degree amplitude in the x-direction. Both phases are Newtonian fluids, with water having a density of 998.2 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s, while air has a density of 1 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity of 1.48e-05 m²/s. Surface tension effects are neglected (sigma=0).

Initial conditions set the entire domain with zero velocity, zero modified pressure (p_rgh), and air-filled (alpha.water=0). Discretization uses Euler scheme for time derivatives, Gauss linear for gradients, vanLeer for divergence terms involving phase fraction, and corrected linear schemes for laplacian and surface normal gradients. Linear interpolation is applied.

The PIMPLE algorithm uses 2 corrector steps without momentum prediction or non-orthogonal correction. Solvers are configured for phase fraction (1 correction, 3 sub-cycles), velocity (Gauss-Seidel smoothing), and pressure (GAMG/PCG with DIC-based preconditioning, tolerances down to 2e-9 for final pressure). Reference pressure is fixed at 1e5 Pa at point (0.0013, 0.0017, 0.0017), with no relaxation applied to velocity equations.
