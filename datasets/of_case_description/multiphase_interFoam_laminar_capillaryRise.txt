This simulation employs the interFoam solver to model laminar capillary rise of water in a vertical channel under gravity and surface tension. It involves two immiscible, Newtonian phases: water (density 1000 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1e-06 m²/s) and air (density 1 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1.48e-05 m²/s), with a surface tension coefficient of 0.0707106 N/m. Gravity acts downward with a magnitude of 10 m/s². The domain is initially filled with air (α.water = 0) at zero velocity and zero modified pressure (p_rgh). Boundary conditions include an inlet with prescribed water volume fraction (α.water = 1) and pressure-outlet atmosphere; walls enforce a 45° contact angle for the interface via constantAlphaContactAngle, no-slip for velocity, and fixedFluxPressure for pressure.

Discretization uses Euler time scheme, Gauss linear gradients, and corrected Laplacian. Divergence terms apply upwind for velocity and vanLeer scheme for phase fraction. The PIMPLE algorithm performs 3 corrector iterations without momentum prediction or non-orthogonal correction. Solvers include smoothSolver (symGaussSeidel) for velocity and PCG (DIC preconditioner) for pressure systems, with tolerances of 1e-6 for velocity and 1e-7 for pressure. Phase fraction solving utilizes 2 sub-cycles per time step.
