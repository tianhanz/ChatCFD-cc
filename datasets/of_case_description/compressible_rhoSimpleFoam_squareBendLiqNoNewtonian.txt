The case 'squareBendLiqNoNewtonian' uses the rhoSimpleFoam solver for a steady-state simulation of incompressible flow through a square bend channel. The fluid is water (H2O) with laminar, power-law non-Newtonian viscosity behavior (nuMin = 0.001 m²/s, nuMax = 1 m²/s, k = 0.02, n = 0.4), and temperature dependence is included. Boundary conditions are: inlet has a fixed mass flow rate of 5 kg/s (with initial density 1000 kg/m³), outlet has a fixed static pressure of 1e5 Pa, walls are no-slip and fixed at a temperature of 350 K, while the inflow temperature is 300 K.

Spatial discretization uses Gauss linear for gradients (with cell-limited scheme for gradients in divergence terms). Divergence schemes for momentum (phi,U), energy (phi,e), and turbulence quantities employ a bounded linearUpwind scheme with the 'limited' limiter. Laplacian terms use Gauss linear corrected.

Solution control uses the SIMPLE algorithm with 0 non-orthogonal correctors. Relaxation factors are set: pressure (p) at 0.3, velocity (U), energy (e), turbulent kinetic energy (k), and turbulent dissipation rate (epsilon) at 0.7. Solver settings specify: pressure (p) solved with GAMG and GaussSeidel smoother; Velocity (U), energy (e), k, and epsilon solved with smoothSolver and symGaussSeidel smoother. All solvers use an absolute tolerance of 1e-7 and relative tolerance of 0.1. Residual convergence targets: pressure (p) and velocity (U) at 1e-4, energy (e), k, and epsilon at 1e-3.
