Simulate the steady-state, incompressible turbulent airflow around a motorcycle geometry using the `simpleFoam` solver in OpenFOAM. Apply the `kOmegaSST` RANS turbulence model with a Newtonian fluid kinematic viscosity of 1.5e-05 m²/s. The SIMPLE algorithm is utilized for pressure-velocity coupling, configured with one momentum predictor step and consistent velocity-pressure treatment. Solver settings specify GAMG for pressure with tolerance 1e-7 and relaxation factor 0.9 for velocity (U), while smoothSolver is employed for turbulent kinetic energy (k) and specific dissipation rate (omega) with tolerance 1e-8 and relaxation factor 0.7. Boundary conditions define an inlet velocity profile and turbulence quantities, outlet pressure with fixed value condition, no-slip walls on the motorcycle surfaces using `kqRWallFunction` for k and `omegaWallFunction` for omega, with `nutkWallFunction` for eddy viscosity. Initial fields set uniform velocity and pressure, along with uniform turbulence fields k and omega based on initial inlet conditions. Spatial discretization uses second-order schemes: Gauss linear for gradients (cell-limited for U), bounded Gauss linearUpwindV for divergence of velocity, and Gauss linear corrected for laplacians. The wall distance calculation method is meshWave.
