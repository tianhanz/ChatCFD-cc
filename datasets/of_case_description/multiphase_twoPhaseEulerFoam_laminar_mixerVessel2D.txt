This simulation employs the twoPhaseEulerFoam solver to model the laminar interaction between air and water in a 2D mixing vessel. Both phases are defined with specific thermophysical properties: water uses a constant-density perfect fluid model with density 1027 kg/m³ and viscosity 3.645e-4 Pa·s, while air follows the perfect gas law with viscosity 1.84e-05 Pa·s. Phase interactions incorporate Schiller-Naumann drag, constant virtual mass coefficients of 0.5, Ranz-Marshall heat transfer, and surface tension of 0.07 N/m. Bubble/droplet diameters are fixed at 1e-4 m for water and pressure-dependent for air starting at 3e-3 m.

The rotating vessel region uses a Multiple Reference Frame (MRF) with angular velocity 10.472 rad/s around the z-axis. Gravity is disabled. Initial conditions set uniform pressure at 1e5 Pa, stagnant flow for both phases, air volume fraction at 0.5, with temperatures fixed at 300 K (air) and 500 K (water). Spatial discretization uses Gauss linear gradients, corrected Laplacians, and compressive vanLeer schemes for phase fraction transport. Momentum/energy equations apply limitedLinear divergence schemes. Solution algorithms use PIMPLE with 3 outer loops, PCG/GAMG for pressure with tolerance 1e-6, and smoothSolver for velocity/energy fields with tolerance 1e-5. Pressure-velocity coupling applies no relaxation.
