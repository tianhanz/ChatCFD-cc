The RAS/TJunction case models turbulent incompressible flow within a T-shaped pipe geometry using the pimpleFoam solver with the standard k-epsilon turbulence model. The simulation assumes Newtonian fluid behavior with a kinematic viscosity of 1e-05 m²/s. Initial conditions set velocity and turbulent viscosity fields to zero throughout the domain. Turbulent kinetic energy (k) and dissipation rate (epsilon) are initialized uniformly at 0.2 m²/s² and 200 m²/s³, respectively.

Boundary conditions are configured as follows: The inlet uses a prescribed total pressure condition with a time-dependent profile and specifies turbulence via a 5% intensity for k and a mixing length of 0.01 m for epsilon. Both outlets enforce fixed static pressure values. Walls apply no-slip velocity conditions and utilize wall functions for turbulence quantities (epsilonWallFunction for epsilon, kqRWallFunction for k, nutkWallFunction for nut).

Numerical schemes employ an Euler time scheme and Gauss linear discretization for gradients. Velocity convection uses a limitedLinearV divergence scheme with a coefficient of 1, while turbulence transport terms apply limitedLinear divergence. Pressure is solved using the GAMG algorithm with a relative tolerance of 0.01, and velocity/turbulence equations utilize smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel smoother and 0.1 relative tolerance. The PIMPLE algorithm executes 1 outer loop and 2 pressure-velocity correctors per time step, with all relaxation factors set to 1.0.
