This simulation aims to model three-dimensional laminar sloshing within a partially water-filled tank that undergoes complex rigid body motion (6 degrees of freedom) using the interFoam solver. The flow involves two immiscible, incompressible Newtonian fluids: water (density 998.2 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1e-06 m²/s) and air (density 1 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1.48e-05 m²/s). Surface tension effects are neglected (sigma = 0). Gravity acts in the negative z-direction (-9.81 m/s²). The tank's prescribed motion is defined by tabulated data.

The initial conditions set the entire domain at rest: velocity (U) is zero everywhere, the modified pressure (p_rgh) is uniformly zero, and the water volume fraction (alpha.water) is uniformly zero initially. The domain boundaries represent solid walls. The simulation utilizes the PIMPLE algorithm with momentum prediction disabled, 2 corrector steps, and pressure-velocity coupling executed without non-orthogonal correction or additional phi correction. Discretization schemes include the Euler method for time derivatives (ddt), Gauss linear for gradients (grad), vanLeer or vanLeerV schemes for divergence terms related to density flux, velocity, and phase fraction (div), Gauss linear corrected for Laplacians (laplacian), linear interpolation (interpolation), and corrected surface normal gradients (snGrad). Solvers include PCG and GAMG for pressure-related equations (using DIC and GaussSeidel smoothers), and a smoothSolver (GaussSeidel smoother) for velocity. The phase fraction solution employs 1 corrector step and 3 sub-cycles per time step.
