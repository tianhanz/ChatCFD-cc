Utilizing the pisoFoam solver for incompressible turbulent flow simulation in a lid-driven cavity configuration with coupled velocity solution. The case employs the k-epsilon RANS turbulence model where turbulence is enabled, and fluid properties specify Newtonian behavior with kinematic viscosity ν = 1e-05 m²/s. The 2D cavity features a moving top wall with fixed x-direction velocity (1 m/s), stationary side walls, and empty front/back boundaries. Initial conditions set internal velocity to zero, pressure to zero, turbulent kinetic energy k to 0.00325 m²/s², dissipation rate epsilon to 0.000765 m²/s³, and turbulent viscosity nut to zero.  

Discretization schemes use Euler time integration, Gauss linear gradients, and limitedLinearV 1 divergence for momentum. Turbulence terms (k, epsilon) apply limitedLinear divergence. Laplacian terms use linear corrected schemes with non-orthogonal correction, and surface-normal gradients are corrected.  

Linear solvers specify GAMG with Gauss-Seidel smoother for pressure, coupled PBiCCCG with DILU preconditioner for velocity, and smoothSolver for turbulence variables. PISO algorithm performs 2 corrector steps without non-orthogonal correction. Boundary conditions enforce fixedValue velocity on the moving lid, no-slip on fixed walls, epsilonWallFunction/kqRWallFunction for turbulence walls, and zeroGradient for pressure.
