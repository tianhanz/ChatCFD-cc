Simulate a two-dimensional laminar shock tube flow using the sonicFoam solver for transient compressible flow. The fluid is a perfect gas with constant properties: specific heat Cp=1005 J/kg·K, dynamic viscosity μ=0, and Prandtl number Pr=0.7. Initial conditions include uniform zero pressure, zero velocity, and temperature of 1 K with zero-gradient boundaries on the sides and empty conditions elsewhere. 

Discretization uses Euler scheme for time derivatives, Gauss linear for gradients, and upwind for momentum convection. Pressure and energy convection employ limitedLinear divergence schemes. The pressure-velocity coupling applies the PIMPLE algorithm with 2 outer correctors and 1 inner corrector. Solvers include smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel smoother for pressure, velocity, and energy fields, and PCG with DIC preconditioner for density. Laminar flow modeling excludes turbulence.
