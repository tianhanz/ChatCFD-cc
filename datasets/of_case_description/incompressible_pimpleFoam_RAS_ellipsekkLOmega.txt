Simulate incompressible turbulent flow around a 2D elliptical obstacle using the pimpleFoam solver with kkLOmega turbulence modeling. The Newtonian fluid has a kinematic viscosity of 1.86e-05 m²/s. Initial conditions set uniform velocity at 3.1 m/s in the streamwise direction, zero pressure, zero turbulent kinetic energy components (kl and kt), specific dissipation rate (omega) at 1e-5 s⁻¹, and zero turbulent viscosity.

Boundaries include velocity inlet (3.1 m/s), pressure outlet (fixed at 0), symmetry at the top, no-slip wall at the obstacle, and empty front/back planes. Discretization uses Euler time schemes, Gauss linear gradients, linearUpwindV divergence for momentum, limitedLinear divergence for turbulence terms, and corrected snGrad schemes. Solution settings apply PIMPLE algorithm with 2 correctors and 1 non-orthogonal corrector per iteration, GAMG solver for pressure (relative tolerance 0.01), and smoothSolver for velocity/turbulence variables with symmetric Gauss-Seidel smoothing. All equations use relaxation factor 1.0.
