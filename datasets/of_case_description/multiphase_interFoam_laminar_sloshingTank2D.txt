Employing the interFoam solver to conduct a 2-dimensional simulation of a laminar sloshing tank without turbulence modeling. The system involves two Newtonian fluids: water (density 998.2 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1e-06 m²/s) and air (density 1 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1.48e-05 m²/s), with surface tension coefficient (sigma) set to zero. The mesh motion is defined using a solidBody motion solver with SDA function parameters for tank oscillation. Initial conditions set the water volume fraction (alpha.water) uniformly to zero (air-filled), velocity (U) uniformly to (0 0 0) m/s, and modified pressure (p_rgh) uniformly to 0 Pa across the domain. Boundary walls use a movingWallVelocity condition for velocity and fixedFluxPressure for modified pressure. The temporal discretization employs the Euler scheme. Spatial discretization utilizes Gauss linear for gradients, corrected Gauss linear for Laplacians, and vanLeer schemes for divergence terms (div(rhoPhi,U), div(phi,alpha), div(phirb,alpha)). The pressure field (p_rgh) is solved using the GAMG solver with tolerance 1e-08 and relTol 0.01, with a PCG final corrector. Velocity is solved using a smoothSolver with GaussSeidel smoother. The PIMPLE algorithm is configured without momentum prediction, executing 2 correctors per time step and disabling non-orthogonal correction and velocity-potential correction, setting a reference pressure point and value. Equations for velocity utilize no relaxation (factor of 1).
