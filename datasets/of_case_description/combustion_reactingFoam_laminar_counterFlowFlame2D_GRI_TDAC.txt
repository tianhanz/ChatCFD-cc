Employ the reactingFoam solver to conduct a 2D laminar counter-flow methane-air flame simulation utilizing GRI-Mech 3.0 chemistry and TDAC (Tabulation of Dynamic Adaptive Chemistry) for combustion modeling. The setup features two opposing inlets: a fuel stream (pure CH4 at 293 K with velocity 0.1 m/s) and an oxidizer stream (23% O₂/77% N₂ at 293 K with velocity -0.1 m/s), meeting at a stagnation plane. An outlet with total pressure boundary condition (1e5 Pa) completes the domain.  

Key discretization schemes:  
- Temporal: Euler implicit  
- Gradient: Gauss linear  
- Divergence: limitedLinear for velocity and species fluxes  
- Laplacian: Gauss linear orthogonal  

Solution algorithms:  
- Pressure solver: PCG with DIC preconditioner (tol 1e⁻⁶, relTol 0.1)  
- Velocity/species/enthalpy solvers: PBiCGStab with DILU preconditioner (tol 1e⁻⁶, relTol 0.1)  
- PIMPLE settings: 1 outer iteration, 2 pressure corrections, no non-orthogonal correction  

Chemistry acceleration:  
- TDAC reduction method: DAC with tolerance 1e⁻⁴ and initial species set {CO, CH₄, HO₂}  
- ISAT tabulation: tolerance 1e⁻⁴, max 2000 leafs, with species scaling  

Initial fields:  
- Uniform pressure 1e5 Pa  
- Uniform temperature 2000 K  
- Species mass fractions: CH₄=0 (except fuel inlet), O₂=0 (except oxidizer inlet), N₂=1 in domain
