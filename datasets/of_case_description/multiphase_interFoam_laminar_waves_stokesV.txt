Employing the interFoam solver, perform a laminar simulation of Stokes fifth-order wave propagation in a multiphase system containing water and air. Both phases are Newtonian fluids: water with density 1000 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity 1e-06 m²/s, air with density 1 kg/m³ and viscosity 1.48e-05 m²/s. Surface tension is set to 0.07 N/m. Initial conditions define the entire domain filled with air (alpha.water=0), zero velocity, and zero modified pressure (p_rgh). Gravity acts downward with magnitude 9.81 m/s².

Wave generation at the inlet uses a StokesV model with wave height 0.1 m, wave period 2.0 s, and linear ramp-up over 4 seconds with active wave absorption. The outlet applies shallow water absorption boundary conditions. Ground is a stationary wall with no-slip velocity and fixedFlux pressure. The top boundary uses pressureInletOutlet for velocity and totalPressure for p_rgh.

Discretization employs Euler time scheme. Gradient terms use Gauss linear. Divergence schemes apply Gauss linearUpwind grad(U) for momentum flux, and compressive vanLeer for volume fraction advection. Pressure-velocity coupling follows the PIMPLE algorithm with 2 correctors and disabled momentum prediction. Solvers include PCG with DIC for pressure, and PBiCG with DILU for velocity, all with absolute tolerance ≤1e-6. The alpha field solver uses 3 sub-cycles per step with interface compression.
