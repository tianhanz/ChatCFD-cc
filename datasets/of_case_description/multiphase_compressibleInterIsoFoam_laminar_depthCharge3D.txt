This case employs the compressibleInterIsoFoam solver to simulate a 3D laminar two-phase flow interaction involving water and air, representing a depth charge scenario without turbulence modeling. The two immiscible phases are governed by separate thermophysical properties: water is modeled as an incompressible perfect fluid with density 1027 kg/m³ and viscosity 3.645e-4 Pa·s, while air follows the ideal gas law with viscosity 1.84e-05 Pa·s and a surface tension coefficient of 0.07 N/m. Gravity acts in the negative y-direction at -9.81 m/s². Initial conditions define the domain as entirely air (alpha.water = 0), uniform pressure (1e5 Pa), zero velocity, and uniform temperature (300 K). 

Discretization uses Euler for time derivatives, Gauss linear for gradients, and upwind schemes for divergence terms. Interface capturing utilizes the isoAlpha reconstruction scheme with VOF method. Solution settings employ the PIMPLE algorithm with 1 outer and 3 pressure correction loops. Solver choices include GAMG with DIC smoother for modified pressure (p_rgh), and smooth solvers for velocity and scalar fields. The simulation maintains mass conservation with pressure-velocity coupling, and the laminar stress term is discretized using a linear scheme.
