This is a laminar dam break simulation using the interMixingFoam solver, modeling the interaction of three Newtonian fluid phases: air, water, and an additional "other" fluid.
Key fluid properties are:
- Air: Density = 1 kg/m³, Kinematic viscosity = 1.48e-05 m²/s
- Water: Density = 1000 kg/m³, Kinematic viscosity = 1e-06 m²/s
- Other fluid: Density = 1010 kg/m³, Kinematic viscosity = 1e-06 m²/s
Surface tension coefficients are 0.05 N/m between air/water and 0.04 N/m between air/other fluid. Gravity is applied downward with a magnitude of 9.81 m/s². The turbulence model is explicitly set to laminar.
Initial conditions define air with volume fraction alpha.air = 1 in the atmosphere region, while alpha.water and alpha.other are uniformly zero. Velocity is initially zero everywhere, and the modified pressure p_rgh is also initially zero. Boundary conditions enforce no-slip velocity and fixedFluxPressure on walls, with inletOutlet boundary conditions for phase fractions at the atmosphere boundary.
Solution settings use the Euler scheme for time derivatives, Gauss limitedLinearV for momentum convection, and the vanLeer scheme for phase fraction advection. The PIMPLE algorithm employs 3 pressure correction iterations without non-orthogonal correction, with Gauss-Seidel/SymGauss-Seidel smoothers employed for velocity and phase fraction fields. The PCG solver with DIC preconditioning is used for pressure equations with specified tolerances.
