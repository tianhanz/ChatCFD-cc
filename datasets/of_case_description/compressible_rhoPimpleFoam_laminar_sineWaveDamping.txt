Conduct a laminar compressible flow simulation of an acoustic sine wave propagation and damping in air using the rhoPimpleFoam solver. The initial domain has uniform static pressure (101325 Pa), zero velocity, and 300 K temperature. An acoustic damping source activates at 0.004 seconds in a spherical shell region centered at (-1.25, 0, 0) with inner radius 1.2 m and outer radius 1.65 m. The inlet boundary imposes a sinusoidal pressure variation (3000 Hz frequency, 50 Pa amplitude), while the outlet uses a wave-transmissive condition. Air is modeled as a perfect gas with Sutherland viscosity, constant specific heat (1007 J/kg·K), and zero formation enthalpy.

Spatial discretization employs linear schemes for gradients and Laplacians, linear upwind for velocity convection, and limited linear for enthalpy convection. Backward differencing handles temporal discretization. Pressure equations solve via PCG with DIC preconditioner, while velocity and enthalpy use smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel. The PIMPLE algorithm applies momentum prediction and allows maximum density variation of 1.5, executing 7 outer correctors and 1 pressure corrector per iteration. Front/back boundaries are treated as empty.
