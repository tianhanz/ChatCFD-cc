The twoPhaseEulerFoam solver models a multiphase bubbly column flow using Large Eddy Simulation (LES) for turbulence. The system consists of air bubbles dispersed in a continuous water phase, influenced by gravity in the negative vertical direction. Thermophysical models define water as an incompressible perfect fluid and air as an ideal gas. Interphase forces include Schiller-Naumann drag, constant virtual mass, and Ranz-Marshall heat transfer models. The simulation initializes air volume fraction uniformly at zero except at the inlet. Discretization employs an Euler scheme for time derivatives, Gauss linear gradients, vanLeer convection for phase fraction, and limitedLinear schemes for momentum/energy. Solution settings utilize PIMPLE pressure-velocity coupling with 3 outer correctors, GAMG/DIC solvers for pressure, and smoothSolver/symGaussSeidel for velocity/turbulence variables without relaxation. Boundary conditions enforce fixed velocity, temperature, and turbulent quantities at the inlet; pressure and phase fraction conditions at the outlet; and no-slip walls with zero-gradient temperature. Turbulent kinetic energy and dissipation rates initialize uniformly with wall functions applied.
