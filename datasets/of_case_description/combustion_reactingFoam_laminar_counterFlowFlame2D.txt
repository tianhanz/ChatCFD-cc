This simulation employs the reactingFoam solver to model a two-dimensional laminar counter-flow methane-air diffusion flame. The domain features separate fuel (pure methane) and oxidizer (23% oxygen, 77% nitrogen) inlets with opposing velocities of 0.1 m/s and -0.1 m/s respectively, an outlet with fixed pressure, and adiabatic front/back boundaries using the empty condition. Initial fields set temperature uniformly at 2000K, pressure at 1e5 Pa, species mass fractions (CH4=1 at fuel inlet, O2=0.23/N2=0.77 at oxidizer inlet), and velocity to zero. 

The chemistry model defines a single-step methane reaction (CH4 + 2O2 → CO2 + 2H2O) using Arrhenius kinetics with pre-exponential factor 5.2e16 and activation temperature 14906 K. Thermophysical properties use JANAF thermodynamics, Sutherland transport, and perfect gas equations. Spatial discretization applies Gauss-limitedLinear schemes for divergence terms (with coefficient 1), linear orthogonal gradients, and Euler temporal discretization. 

Solution algorithms employ PIMPLE with 2 pressure-velocity correctors per outer iteration and momentum prediction disabled. Pressure solves use PCG/DIC with relative tolerance 0.1, while velocity and scalars use PBiCGStab/DILU with relative tolerance 0.1. A post-processing function calculates Bilger mixture fraction at every time step. The simulation requires tracking species transport, reaction kinetics, and heat release in a laminar counter-flow flame without turbulence modeling.
