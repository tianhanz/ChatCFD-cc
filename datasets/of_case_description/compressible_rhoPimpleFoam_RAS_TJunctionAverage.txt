This simulation employs the rhoPimpleFoam solver to model compressible turbulent flow through a T-junction, using the k-epsilon turbulence model. The working fluid is air, treated as a perfect gas with constant specific heat and Sutherland's law for viscosity. The discretization utilizes Euler time integration, Gauss linear gradients, and limitedLinear schemes for divergence terms involving velocity (U), enthalpy (h), and turbulence quantities. Wall functions are applied for k, epsilon, and nut near walls. The PIMPLE algorithm is configured with 1 outer corrector, 5 pressure correctors, and no non-orthogonal correctors. Solvers use GAMG for pressure and smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel for U, k, epsilon, and h, with specific tolerances and relaxation factors defined.

Boundary conditions include a pressure-driven inlet specified via a uniformTotalPressure type with a pressure table, turbulent intensity (5%) for k, and a mixing length for epsilon. One outlet employs a custom expression to set pressure based on average inlet and adjacent outlet values. Fixed-value conditions are applied to the other outlet. No-slip walls use fixedValue velocity. Heat addition at the inlet is modelled using outletMappedUniformInletHeatAddition with a specified heat flux (5W). A viscous dissipation model is also activated. Initial fields set uniform pressure (1e5 Pa), zero velocity, temperature (300K), k (1 m²/s²), epsilon (200 m²/s³), nut (0), and alphat (0.001 kg/(m·s)).
