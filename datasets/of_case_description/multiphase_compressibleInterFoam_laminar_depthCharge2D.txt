This simulation models a 2D depth charge scenario using the compressibleInterFoam solver for laminar multiphase flow with water and air. Both phases are Newtonian fluids. Water has constant density ρ=1027 kg/m³, viscosity μ=3.645e-4 Pa·s, and specific heat Cp=4195 J/kg·K. Air is modeled as a perfect gas with μ=1.84e-5 Pa·s and Cp=1007 J/kg·K. Surface tension σ=0.07 N/m is applied at the interface. Gravity acts downward with g=-9.81 m/s².

Initial conditions include uniform air filling (alpha.water=0), stationary flow (U=0), uniform pressure p=1e5 Pa, modified pressure p_rgh=1e5 Pa, and temperature T=300 K. Wall boundaries use no-slip velocity, zero-gradient pressure, and fixed-flux pressure for p_rgh.

The solution employs Euler time discretization and finite-volume spatial schemes: Gauss linear gradients, upwind divergence for convective terms, and Gauss uncorrected laplacian. The PIMPLE algorithm uses 1 outer and 2 inner correctors without momentum prediction. Linear solvers include GAMG/PCG for pressure with DIC preconditioning, smoothSolver with Gauss-Seidel for velocity, and sub-cycling with 5 limiter iterations for phase fraction. Absolute tolerances range from 1e-8 to 1e-5 across variables.
