Simulate 3D conductive heat transfer through a solid structure using the solidFoam solver. This models steady-state thermal diffusion without radiation effects, as confirmed in radiationProperties. The solid material has constant thermophysical properties: density of 8000 kg/m³, specific heat capacity of 450 J/kg·K, and thermal conductivity of 80 W/m·K, defined in thermophysicalProperties.

The initial temperature field is uniformly set to 400 K. Boundary conditions for temperature (T) are specified: the bottom face is fixed at 400 K, the top face uses a turbulent temperature radiative coupling model with neighbor field reference, while all walls are insulated (zero heat flux, zeroGradient). The pressure field is initialized uniformly to 1e5 Pa with calculated boundaries.

Discretization employs the Euler scheme for temporal derivatives and Gauss linear schemes for spatial gradients and interpolation. The solver PCG with DIC preconditioner targets the enthalpy equation ("h.*") with a tolerance of 1e-06 and zero relative tolerance. Non-orthogonal correctors are disabled in PIMPLE settings, and uncorrected schemes handle surface normal gradients and laplacian terms.
