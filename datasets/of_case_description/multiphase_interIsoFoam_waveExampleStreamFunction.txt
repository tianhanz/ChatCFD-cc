Simulate a surface wave scenario using the interIsoFoam solver for two incompressible, immiscible Newtonian fluids (water and air) under laminar flow conditions. The simulation models wave generation via the streamFunction wave model at the inlet, specifying a wave height of 0.1517 m and wave period of 3.017 s. Active wave absorption is enabled at the outlet using the shallowWaterAbsorption model. Gravitational acceleration is set to 9.81 m/s² in the downward z-direction.

The fluids’ properties are defined: water density is 1000 kg/m³ with kinematic viscosity 1e-06 m²/s; air density is 1 kg/m³ with kinematic viscosity 1.48e-05 m²/s. Surface tension is 0.07 N/m.

Discretization employs the Euler scheme for time derivatives. Gradient terms use Gauss linear interpolation. Divergence of momentum (div(rhoPhi,U)) applies the Gauss linearUpwind scheme. Laplacian terms use Gauss linear orthogonal, and surface normal gradients use orthogonal scheme. Interface capturing uses the plicRDF reconstruction scheme with clipping enabled.

Solver settings include:
- Pressure (p_rgh): solved with PCG and DIC preconditioner, tolerance 1e-8, relTol 0.05.
- Velocity (U): solved with smoothSolver (symGaussSeidel smoother), tolerance 1e-6.
- PIMPLE algorithm: momentum prediction off, 1 outer correction, 3 pressure-velocity correctors, no non-orthogonal correction. Relaxation factors are set to 1 globally.

Initial conditions set velocity (U), pressure (p_rgh), and water volume fraction (alpha.water) to zero throughout the domain. Boundary conditions utilize waveVelocity for velocity and waveAlpha for water fraction at inlet/outlet. Ground uses fixedValue zero velocity and fixedFluxPressure for pressure. The top boundary applies pressureInletOutletVelocity for velocity and totalPressure for pressure.
