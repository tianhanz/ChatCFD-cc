The squareBend case simulates compressible turbulent flow through a 90-degree duct bend using the rhoSimpleFoam solver with k-epsilon turbulence modeling. This steady-state simulation involves air (modeled as a perfect gas with Sutherland viscosity, constant Cp=1007 J/kg·K, and molecular weight 28.9 g/mol). Key boundary conditions include a mass-flow inlet (0.5 kg/s) with turbulent intensity specification, zero-gradient pressure at walls, fixed pressure outlet, and adiabatic walls.

Discretization uses second-order schemes: Gauss linear for gradients and Gauss linear corrected for Laplacian terms. Convective fluxes are calculated using bounded Gauss upwind for momentum, energy, and turbulence equations. The SIMPLE algorithm solves coupled equations for pressure (GAMG solver) and velocity/turbulence quantities with 0 non-orthogonal correctors.

Relaxation factors are applied (U:0.9, k:0.9, epsilon:0.9) with residual controls (p≤1e-3, U≤1e-4). Initial fields set velocity to zero, static pressure to 110 kPa, turbulence kinetic energy to 1 m²/s², dissipation rate to 200 m²/s³, and temperature to 1000 K uniformly. Wall functions enforce turbulence closure at walls via nutkWallFunction and epsilonWallFunction with standard coefficients (Cμ=0.09, κ=0.41, E=9.8).
