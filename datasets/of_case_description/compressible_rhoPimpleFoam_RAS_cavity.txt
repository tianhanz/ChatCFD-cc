This simulation models a compressible lid-driven cavity flow using the rhoPimpleFoam solver with the k-Omega SST turbulence model in a 2D domain. The top wall moves tangentially at 1 m/s in the x-direction while the other walls are stationary. Initial pressure is 100000 Pa, initial temperature is 300 K, and the fluid is air modeled as a perfect gas with constant properties. The PIMPLE algorithm includes momentum prediction and limits pressure between 80000 Pa and 120000 Pa. Temporal discretization uses the Euler scheme. Spatial gradients use Gauss linear scheme, while divergence terms for velocity and turbulence variables employ limited linear schemes. Pressure equations utilize the PCG solver with DIC preconditioner and 1e-6 tolerance. Velocity and turbulence equations use the smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel smoother, tolerances of 1e-5, and limitedLinear divergence discretization for stability. Wall functions are applied to turbulence quantities near boundaries.
