Utilize the interFoam solver to simulate turbulent water channel flow with a free surface interaction between water and air. The simulation employs the kOmegaSST RAS turbulence model for capturing turbulent effects. Water (density 1000 kg/m³, viscosity 1e-06 m²/s) and air (density 1 kg/m³, viscosity 1.48e-05 m²/s) are modeled as Newtonian fluids with a surface tension coefficient of 0.07 N/m under gravitational acceleration (0, 0, -9.81 m/s²). Initial conditions set the entire domain to air (alpha.water = 0), with uniform velocity (1, 0, 0) m/s, modified pressure (p_rgh) at 0 Pa, turbulence kinetic energy (k) at 0.0001 m²/s², and specific dissipation rate (omega) at 0.003 s⁻¹.

Spatial discretization uses linear Gaussian schemes for gradients, vanLeer for divergence of volume fraction, and linearUpwind for momentum equations. First-order Euler scheme handles temporal discretization. The PIMPLE algorithm executes 2 pressure-velocity corrector steps without momentum prediction or non-orthogonal correction. Solvers include GAMG for pressure (relative tolerance 0.01) and smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel for velocity/turbulence fields (relative tolerance 0.1). Volume fraction advection uses MULES correction with 3 limiter iterations. Boundary conditions feature flow-rate inlet (50 m³/s), atmosphere pressure boundary, no-slip walls, and turbulence wall functions.
