This simulation employs the buoyantBoussinesqSimpleFoam solver to model steady-state buoyancy-driven airflow in a heated room using the Boussinesq approximation. It utilizes the k-epsilon turbulence model with wall functions. The fluid is Newtonian with kinematic viscosity 1e-05 m²/s. Gravity acts downward with acceleration 9.81 m/s². Thermal properties include a reference temperature of 300 K, thermal expansion coefficient 0.003 K⁻¹, laminar Prandtl number 0.7, and turbulent Prandtl number 0.85. Initial conditions set uniform temperature at 300 K, zero velocity, modified pressure (p_rgh) at 0 Pa, turbulence kinetic energy (k) at 0.1 m²/s², turbulent dissipation rate (epsilon) at 0.01 m²/s³, and turbulent viscosity (nut) at 0 m²/s.

Boundary conditions specify no-slip walls for velocity, fixed-value temperature (300 K) at ceiling/floor with adiabatic walls elsewhere. Wall functions are applied for turbulence quantities: kqRWallFunction for k, epsilonWallFunction for epsilon, nutkWallFunction for nut, and alphatJayatillekeWallFunction for thermal diffusivity. Pressure boundaries use fixedFluxPressure with rhok density coupling.

Discretization employs steady-state time derivatives. Convection schemes use bounded Gauss upwind for velocity (phi,U), temperature (phi,T), and turbulence fields. Diffusion terms apply Gauss linear corrected Laplacian schemes. Solution control utilizes the SIMPLE algorithm with relaxation factors (pressure: 0.7, velocity: 0.3, temperature: 0.5) and residual tolerances (p_rgh: 1e-2, U: 1e-4, T: 1e-2, turbulence: 1e-3).
