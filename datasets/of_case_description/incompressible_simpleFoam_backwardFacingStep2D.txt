The simulation focuses on a 2D backward-facing step turbulent flow using the steady-state solver simpleFoam with the k-Omega SST turbulence model. A Newtonian fluid with a kinematic viscosity of 1.56e-05 m²/s is modeled. The inlet velocity is uniformly set to 44.2 m/s in the streamwise direction, while turbulence quantities (k, epsilon, omega) are initialized using fixed-value inlet conditions and wall functions at boundaries. Pressure is configured with zero-gradient at the inlet and a fixed value of zero at the outlet. Symmetry planes are applied to side boundaries, and no-slip conditions are enforced on walls.

Spatial discretization employs Gauss linear schemes for gradients and corrected linear schemes for Laplacian terms. Divergence schemes include bounded LUST for velocity and limited linear schemes for turbulence terms. The SIMPLE algorithm solves the incompressible RANS equations with consistent pressure-velocity coupling and zero non-orthogonal correctors. GAMG solver with DICGaussSeidel smoother addresses pressure, while smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel handles velocity and turbulence variables. Relaxation factors of 0.9 for velocity and 0.8 for other fields stabilize convergence. Residual tolerances are 1e-4 for pressure, 1e-6 for velocity, and 1e-5 for turbulence variables.
