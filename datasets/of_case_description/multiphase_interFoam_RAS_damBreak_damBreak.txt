This simulation models a dam-break scenario with turbulent two-phase flow involving water and air using the interFoam solver in OpenFOAM. The simulation employs the k-epsilon turbulence model and considers gravity acting vertically downward (-Y direction). Both fluids are Newtonian, with water defined by a density of 1000 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity of 1e-06 m²/s, and air by a density of 1 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity of 1.48e-05 m²/s. A surface tension coefficient of 0.07 N/m is used between the phases.

Key solver settings include PIMPLE for pressure-velocity coupling with momentum prediction disabled, 1 outer correction and 3 inner pressure corrections per time step, and no relaxation applied to the equations. The phase fraction equation is solved using the MULES method with 2 alpha corrections per time step and 3 limiter iterations. The pressure equation (p_rgh) and phase fraction equation use the Preconditioned Conjugate Gradient (PCG) solver. Momentum (U), turbulence kinetic energy (k), and turbulence dissipation rate (epsilon) equations use a smooth solver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel smoother.

Discretization employs a Euler scheme for time derivatives. Gradient terms use Gauss linear interpolation. Convection of the phase fraction is discretized with the van Leer scheme, while velocity convection uses Gauss linearUpwind. Turbulence scalars k and epsilon convection uses upwind scheme. Divergence of stress terms use Gauss linear. Laplacian terms use Gauss linear orthogonal. Pressure and velocity gradients at faces use orthogonal correction.

Initial conditions are set throughout the domain: Velocity (U) is zero everywhere; Volume fraction of water (alpha.water) is uniformly zero; Modified pressure (p_rgh) is zero; Turbulence scalars k and epsilon are initialized to 0.1; Turbulent viscosity (nut) is zero. Wall boundaries (leftWall, rightWall, lowerWall) apply no-slip velocity conditions, fixedFluxPressure for p_rgh, and wall functions (epsilonWallFunction, kqRWallFunction, nutkWallFunction) for turbulence quantities. The atmosphere boundary uses inletOutlet conditions for phase fraction and turbulence scalars (outlet if outward flow, otherwise uniform inlet value), pressureInletOutletVelocity for velocity, and totalPressure for p_rgh.
