Simulate a 2-dimensional incompressible laminar lid-driven cavity flow using the clipped cavity geometry and the icoFoam solver. The top wall ("lid") moves tangentially with a constant velocity of 1 m/s in the x-direction, while the remaining walls ("fixedWalls") are stationary no-slip surfaces. The "frontAndBack" planes use an empty boundary condition. The fluid has a constant kinematic viscosity (nu) of 0.01 m²/s. The initial state is fluid at rest (velocity U = 0 m/s everywhere) and a relative pressure (p) of 0 m²/s² throughout the domain.

The transient simulation employs an Euler implicit scheme (ddtSchemes) for time integration. Spatial discretization uses a standard Gauss linear scheme for gradients (gradSchemes) and for the divergence of the velocity flux (div(phi,U)). The laplacian and snGrad schemes are set for orthogonal meshes. For the pressure equation, the solver is Preconditioned Conjugate Gradient (PCG) with a Diagonal Incomplete Cholesky (DIC) preconditioner, a tolerance of 1e-06, and a relative tolerance (relTol) of 0.05. A final pressure solution step uses zero relTol. The velocity equation solver is smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel smoother, a tolerance of 1e-05, and zero relTol. The PISO algorithm is used for pressure-velocity coupling with 2 correctors and zero non-orthogonal correctors. A pressure reference cell is set to zero.
