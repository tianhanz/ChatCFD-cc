This simulation employs the interFoam solver to model a laminar dam break flow interaction with a permeable boundary using the k-epsilon turbulence model. The two-phase system comprises water (density 1000 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1e-06 m²/s) and air (density 1 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1.48e-05 m²/s), with a surface tension coefficient of 0.07 N/m. Gravity acts vertically downward at 9.81 m/s².

Initial conditions set the water volume fraction (alpha.water) to 0 throughout the domain, indicating an initially air-filled cavity. Both velocity and modified pressure (p_rgh) fields initialize uniformly to zero. Turbulent kinetic energy (k) and dissipation rate (epsilon) initialize to 0.1 m²/s² and m²/s³ respectively, while turbulent viscosity (nut) starts at zero.

Boundary specifications include a permeable surface (rightWall) utilizing specialized alphaPressure and velocity conditions for water-air interaction. Solid walls (leftWall, lowerWall) impose no-slip velocity and fixedFlux pressure constraints. An atmosphere boundary allows bidirectional flow with pressure-driven conditions.

Solver configurations use PIMPLE algorithm without momentum prediction, performing 1 outer loop and 3 inner corrections. The alpha.water equation utilizes MULES compression with Smooth solver, while p_rgh employs PCG with DIC preconditioner (relative tolerance 0.05). Turbulence equations (U/k/epsilon) apply Smooth solver iterations.

Discretization employs Euler temporal schemes. Spatial discretization features:  
- Gradients: Gauss linear  
- Divergence: vanLeer for phase fraction, linearUpwind for momentum  
- Laplacians: orthogonal scheme  
- Flux limiting for phase fraction preservation.  
No non-orthogonal correction is applied during the solution process.
