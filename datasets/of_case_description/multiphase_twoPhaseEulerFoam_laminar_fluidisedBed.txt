This simulation employs the twoPhaseEulerFoam solver to model laminar fluidization of solid particles in air within a fluidized bed. The primary phases are solid "particles" (initial volume fraction uniformly zero) and continuous "air" (initial vertical velocity 0.25 m/s). Gravity acts downwards with -9.81 m/s². Pressure (p and p_rgh) initial fields are uniformly 1e5 Pa. Initial temperatures are uniformly 600 K for both phases.

Key discretization schemes include Gauss linear for gradients and Gauss vanLeer for divergence of volume fraction fields. Velocity divergence utilizes Gauss limitedLinearV 1. Default temporal discretization is Euler (ddtSchemes).

The PIMPLE algorithm solves the transient equations with 3 outer correction loops and 1 pressure-velocity corrector per loop. Solvers employ GAMG with DIC smoother for pressure (p_rgh, tolerance 1e-8), while other fields (U.*, alpha.*, (h|e).*, Theta.*, (k|epsilon).*) use the smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel smoother and tolerances ranging from 1e-5 to 1e-9. Explicit relaxation (factor 1) is applied.

Physical interaction models are configured in constant/phaseProperties. Drag uses the GidaspowErgunWenYu model. Virtual mass applies a constant coefficient (Cvm = 0.5). Heat transfer employs the RanzMarshall model. Particle phase dynamics leverage RAS modeling with the phasePressure RAS model and kinetic theory closures. Kinetic theory parameters include particle restitution coefficient (e=0.8), maximum packing (alphaMax=0.62), frictional stress model (JohnsonJackson), and radial distribution model (SinclairJackson). The continuous air phase is treated as laminar flow.

Boundary conditions specify: an interstitialInletVelocity for U.air at the inlet; fixedValue for U.particles and Theta.particles; fixedFluxPressure or prghPressure for p_rgh; calculated/pressureInletOutletVelocity at the outlet; and noSlip walls for air velocity combined with zeroGradient for particle fields. Temperature boundaries include fixedValue for inlet air and zeroGradient elsewhere.
