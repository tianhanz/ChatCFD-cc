This OpenFOAM LES simulation models cavitating, compressible fluid flow through a throttle configuration using the cavitatingFoam solver. The flow involves two Newtonian phases: liquid water (density 830 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 7.831e-06 m²/s) and its vapour (density 0.14 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 4.252e-05 m²/s), with cavitation dynamics governed by a linear barotropic compressibility model and a saturation pressure of 4500 Pa. Turbulence is modeled using LES with the kEqn subgrid model and the vanDriest near-wall damping approach. Initial conditions set uniform pressure at 300 bar, density at 845 kg/m³, turbulent kinetic energy at 10 m²/s², and zero vapour volume fraction and velocity throughout the domain. Boundary conditions include a total pressure inlet at 300 bar, a fixed pressure outlet at 100 bar, no-slip walls with appropriate turbulence wall functions, and symmetry planes. The solution employs the PIMPLE algorithm with 3 correction steps, utilizing GAMG with Gauss-Seidel smoothing for pressure and smoothSolver with symmetric Gauss-Seidel for other variables like velocity, turbulence, and density, with relative tolerances of 0.1 during iterations and tight absolute tolerances for final convergence. Discretization uses Euler for time derivatives, linear interpolation, Gauss linear gradients, linear corrected Laplacians, and specific convection schemes: vanLeer for density and LUST for momentum and turbulence.
