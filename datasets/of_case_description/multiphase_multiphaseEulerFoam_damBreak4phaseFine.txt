This simulation employs the multiphaseEulerFoam solver to model the laminar interaction of four immiscible phases (water, oil, mercury, air) in a dam break scenario. The physical setup involves Newtonian fluids with distinct densities and viscosities: water (rho=1000 kg/m³, nu=1e-06 m²/s), oil (rho=500 kg/m³, nu=1e-06 m²/s), mercury (rho=13529 kg/m³, nu=1.125e-07 m²/s), and air (rho=1 kg/m³, nu=1.48e-05 m²/s). A uniform gravitational acceleration (g = (0, -9.81, 0) m/s²) acts on the system. Surface tension coefficients between air and all liquid phases are set to 0.07 N/m, with interface compression enabled at all phase boundaries. Drag forces between phases are modeled using a blended SchillerNaumann approach. Contact angles on walls are consistently defined as 90 degrees for all phase pairs.

Initial conditions set all phase volume fractions (alpha) and phase velocities (U.water, U.oil, U.mercury, U.air) to zero uniformly within the domain, except at the atmosphere boundary where alpha.air=1. The modified pressure field (p_rgh) is initialized uniformly to zero. Boundary conditions enforce no-slip velocity at walls, flux-corrected velocity at the atmosphere boundary, zero-gradient for phase fractions at walls, multiphaseEuler::alphaContactAngle with defined contact angles for phase fractions at walls, and inletOutlet/inletValue conditions for phases at the atmosphere. Pressure uses fixedFluxPressure at walls and totalPressure at the atmosphere.

Numerically, discretization employs the Euler scheme for time derivatives, Gauss linear for gradients, Gauss vanLeer for divergence terms involving phase fractions, Gauss limitedLinearV (limit 1) for momentum convection terms, and Gauss linear corrected for Laplacians. Pressure equations (p_rgh) are solved using the GAMG solver with GaussSeidel smoother (tolerance 1e-7, relTol 0.05), and a final pressure corrector uses PCG with GAMG preconditioner (tolerance 1e-7). Velocity equations use the smoothSolver with GaussSeidel smoother (tolerance 1e-8, relTol 0.1). The PIMPLE algorithm executes 3 pressure correction steps without non-orthogonal correctors. No turbulence model is active.
