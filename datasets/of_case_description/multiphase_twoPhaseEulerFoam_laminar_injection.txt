The simulation employs the twoPhaseEulerFoam solver to model laminar air injection into a quiescent water-filled domain under the influence of gravity. The system consists of two immiscible phases: air and water. Physical properties define water as a perfect fluid with constant density (1027 kg/m³) and viscosity (3.645e-4 Pa·s), while air is a perfect gas. Gravity acts downward in the y-direction at -9.81 m/s².

A localized injector introduces air at point (0.075 0.2 0.05) starting at time 0.1s for 5 seconds. This adds mass (1e-3 kg/s of air), downward momentum (U.air: (0 -1e-2 0) m/s), and internal energy (e.air: 500 W) to the domain. Phase interactions include Schiller-Naumann drag models and constant virtual mass coefficients (Cvm=0.5) for both air-in-water and water-in-air configurations, with heat transfer modelled via Ranz-Marshall correlations.

Initial conditions set both phases with zero velocity, ambient pressure (1e5 Pa), and uniform temperature (300 K). The water occupies the entire domain initially (alpha.air = 0). Boundary walls enforce no-slip velocity and zero-gradient temperature conditions.

Numerically, the simulation uses an Euler time scheme. Gradients use Gauss linear discretization. For divergence terms: vanLeer is applied to air volume fraction (div(phi,alpha.air)), while limitedLinearV (limiter=1) discretizes momentum convection and limitedLinear (limiter=1) handles energy convection. Pressure (p_rgh) is solved using a GAMG linear solver with DIC smoother (tolerance 1e-8, relTol 0), while velocity ("U.*") and internal energy ("e.*") fields are solved via smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel smoother (tolerance 1e-5 and 1e-8 respectively). A PIMPLE algorithm performs 3 outer correctors with 1 pressure corrector and no non-orthogonal correctors. No relaxation factors are applied to equations.
