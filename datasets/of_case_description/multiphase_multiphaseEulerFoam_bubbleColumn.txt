Performing a laminar multiphase bubble column simulation using the multiphaseEulerFoam solver with air and water phases. Initial conditions set water to uniformly fill the domain (α_water = 1) while air enters at the inlet with α_air = 0.5 and vertical velocity of 0.1 m/s. Gravity acts vertically downward (-y direction) with magnitude 9.81 m/s². The drag model employs blended Schiller-Naumann correlations with virtual mass effects included. Pressure-velocity coupling uses a PIMPLE algorithm with 1 outer loop and 2 pressure correctors.

Discretization schemes include Euler time integration, Gauss linear gradients, vanLeer for phase fraction divergence, and limitedLinearV for momentum. Pressure is solved using GAMG with DIC smoother, while momentum and scalar fields use smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel. No relaxation factors are applied. Boundary conditions feature fixedValue for inlets (velocity/phase fraction), inletOutlet for outlets, no-slip walls, and zeroGradient for scalars on walls. Properties include water density 1000 kg/m³, air density 1 kg/m³, and respective kinematic viscosities of 1e-6 m²/s and 1.6e-5 m²/s. Bubble diameters are constant at 3 mm for air and 0.1 mm for water.
