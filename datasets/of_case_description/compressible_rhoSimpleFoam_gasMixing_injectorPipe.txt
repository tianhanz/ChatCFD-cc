This simulation employs the rhoSimpleFoam solver to analyze compressible, steady-state gas mixing in an injector pipe configuration using OpenFOAM. The flow involves two separate inlet streams: air (mass flow rate = 0.095 kg/s) and fuel (mass flow rate = 0.005 kg/s), mixing within the pipe, and exiting through an outlet maintained at a fixed pressure of 1e5 Pa. A passive scalar tracer (`tracer0`) is used to track fuel distribution (tracer0=0 for air inlet, tracer0=1 for fuel inlet). Walls are treated with no-slip conditions, and the fluid is air modeled as a perfect gas with constant specific heat (Cp = 1007 J/kg/K) and Sutherland viscosity.

The simulation utilizes the k-epsilon RANS turbulence model with wall functions applied to all wall boundaries. Inlet turbulence is specified using turbulent intensity (5% for both inlets to set `k`) and mixing lengths (0.005m for air, 0.001m for fuel to set `epsilon`).

Key discretization settings involve:
*   **Gradient Terms:** Primarily least squares, with cell-limited Gauss linear (0.99) for velocity gradients.
*   **Divergence Terms:** The `limitedLinear` scheme is applied to most advective fluxes, including momentum (`phi,U`), scalars (e.g., `tracer0`, `k`, `epsilon`), and energy-related terms. The linear scheme is used for the Reynolds stress divergence (`div(R)`).
*   **Laplacian Terms:** Gauss linear corrected.
*   **Surface Normal Gradients:** Corrected.

Solver settings employ the SIMPLE algorithm for pressure-velocity coupling (1 pressure correction, 0 non-orthogonal correction). GAMG with a symGaussSeidel smoother solves the pressure (`p`) and density (`rho`) equations. The `smoothSolver` with symGaussSeidel solves velocity (`U`), turbulence quantities (`k`, `epsilon`), and the passive scalar (`tracer0`). PBiCGStab with DILU preconditioning solves the internal energy (`e`) equation. Field relaxation factors are used (e.g., p: 0.3, rho: 0.05, U/k/epsilon: 0.7). Solution tolerances and min/max density clipping (rhoMin=0.3 kg/m³, rhoMax=1.4 kg/m³) are set appropriately for convergence.
