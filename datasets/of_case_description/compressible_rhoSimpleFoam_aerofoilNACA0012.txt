This simulation models the steady-state compressible flow around a NACA0012 airfoil using the rhoSimpleFoam solver. The fluid is air modeled as a perfect gas with constant properties: molecular weight 28.9 g/mol, Cp=1005 J/kg·K, dynamic viscosity 1.82e-05 Pa·s, and Prandtl number 0.71. The freestream inlet velocity is 250 m/s (x-direction), static pressure 1e5 Pa, temperature 298 K, turbulent kinetic energy 0.01 m²/s², and specific dissipation rate 10 1/s. The turbulence model is kOmegaSST, with wall functions applied at the airfoil surface.

The SIMPLE algorithm handles pressure-velocity coupling with under-relaxation factors: pressure 0.7, velocity 0.3, turbulence quantities 0.7. Residual controls are set to 1e-4 for pressure, velocity, and turbulence fields. Temperature is constrained between 101-1000 K globally.

Discretization schemes include:
- Gradients: Cell-limited linear (gradient limiter=1) for U/k/omega
- Divergence: Bounded linear upwind for momentum/energy terms; upwind for turbulence
- Laplacian: Linear with non-orthogonal correction
- Surface normal gradients: Corrected scheme

Solvers configuration:
- Pressure: Geometric-algebraic multigrid (GAMG) with Gauss-Seidel smoother
- Velocity and turbulence: Preconditioned biconjugate gradient (PBiCGStab) with diagonal incomplete LU preconditioning
- Tolerances: Absolute 1e-6, relative tolerances set to 0.01 for pressure and 0.1 for other variables

The simulation outputs aerodynamic forces and flow field data for the airfoil under specified compressible flow conditions.
