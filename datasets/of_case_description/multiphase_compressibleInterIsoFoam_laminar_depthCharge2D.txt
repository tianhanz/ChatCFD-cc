Simulating a 2D laminar compressible two-phase flow for a depth charge case using the compressibleInterIsoFoam solver. The fluids involved are water and air, with surface tension and gravity effects included. Water is modeled as a perfect fluid with density 1027 kg/m³, viscosity 3.645e-4 Pa·s, and specific heat 4195 J/kg·K. Air is modeled as a perfect gas with viscosity 1.84e-5 Pa·s and specific heat 1007 J/kg·K. Surface tension coefficient is 0.07 N/m, and gravity acts downward with -9.81 m/s² in y-direction.

Initial conditions: Uniform water volume fraction of 0 (air-filled), pressure 1e5 Pa, velocity zero, temperature 300 K. Boundary walls use no-slip velocity, fixedFluxPressure for modified pressure, and zero-gradient for temperature/phase fraction. Front/back boundaries are empty (2D).

Discretization uses Euler time scheme, Gauss linear gradients, and upwind divergence for phase/energy/momentum fluxes. Pressure-velocity coupling employs PIMPLE with 1 outer correction, 2 inner corrections, no momentum predictor, and no non-orthogonal correctors. Linear solvers: GAMG/PCG for pressure with DIC smoother (tolerance 1e-7), smoothSolver for velocity with Gauss-Seidel sweeps (tolerance 1e-6), and specialized sub-cycling/reconstruction for phase fraction. Diagonal solver handles density fields. Thermal and turbulence variables use symGaussSeidel smoothSolver with strict 1e-8 tolerance.
