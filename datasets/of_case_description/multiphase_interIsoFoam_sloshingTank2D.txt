This simulation models the sloshing dynamics of a two-dimensional rectangular tank containing water and air phases using the interIsoFoam solver with laminar flow assumptions. The initial water volume fraction (alpha.water) is uniformly zero throughout the domain. Velocity (U) and modified pressure (p_rgh) fields are initially zero. Gravity acts downward with acceleration -9.81 m/s². Both phases are Newtonian fluids: water has density 998.2 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity 1e-06 m²/s, air has density 1 kg/m³ and kinematic viscosity 1.48e-05 m²/s. Surface tension is disabled (sigma=0).

The mesh undergoes prescribed rigid-body motion defined by the SDA ship motion model with parameters including a center of gravity at (0,0,0), roll amplitudes between 0.10472 and 0.22654 rad, heave amplitude 3.79 m, sway amplitude 2.34 m, and motion period parameters of 13.93s. Time discretization uses Euler scheme. Spatial gradients employ linear Gauss integration. Momentum convection applies the vanLeerV scheme for divergence terms with viscous stress discretized linearly. Pressure-velocity coupling uses the PIMPLE algorithm with two corrections and disabled momentum prediction. The alpha.water solution utilizes 3 sub-cycles and isoAlpha reconstruction with phase clipping. Pressure solves use GAMG and PCG solvers with tolerances ranging from 1e-05 to 2e-09, while velocity solves apply Gauss-Seidel smoothing with tolerance 1e-06.
