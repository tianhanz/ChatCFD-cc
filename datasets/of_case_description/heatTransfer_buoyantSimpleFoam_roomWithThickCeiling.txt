The simulation models buoyancy-driven airflow within a room featuring a thick ceiling structure, employing the steady-state solver `buoyantSimpleFoam`. A RANS approach is used with the `kEpsilon` turbulence model. Spatial discretization utilizes the Gauss linear scheme for gradients. Divergence schemes for momentum, energy, and turbulence terms (`phi,U`, `phi,h`, `phi,k`, `phi,epsilon`) apply bounded `limitedLinear` interpolation with a limit coefficient of 0.2. The pressure field (`p_rgh`) is solved using the GAMG algorithm with DICGaussSeidel smoothing and a relaxation factor of 0.7. Velocity (`U`), enthalpy (`h`), and turbulence fields (`k`, `epsilon`) are solved using PBiCGStab with DILU preconditioning, using relaxation factors of 0.3 for `U` and turbulence fields, and 0.8 for `h`.

The working fluid is dry air modelled as a perfect gas with constant properties: molecular weight 28.96 g/mol, specific heat capacity (Cp) 1004.4 J/kg·K, dynamic viscosity 1.831e-05 Pa·s, and Prandtl number 0.705. Gravity acts vertically downward (-y direction) with a magnitude of 9.81 m/s². The ceiling boundary incorporates a conjugate heat transfer model, implemented via the `compressible::thermalBaffle` boundary condition. This couples the fluid domain to a separate solid region ("baffle3DRegion") representing the thick ceiling material, specified as a solid with density 80 kg/m³, specific heat capacity 15 J/kg·K, and thermal conductivity 0.01 W/m·K. The outer surface of this solid ceiling is fixed at 340 K. Pressure (`p_rgh`) on walls uses the `fixedFluxPressure` condition. Turbulent kinetic energy (`k`) and dissipation rate (`epsilon`) utilize `kqRWallFunction` and `epsilonWallFunction` boundary conditions respectively on walls. The initial state assumes uniform static pressure (101325 Pa), uniform temperature (300 K) in the fluid domain, and uniform turbulent kinetic energy (0.1 m²/s²).
