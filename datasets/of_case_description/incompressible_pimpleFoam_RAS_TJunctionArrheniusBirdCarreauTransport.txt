Conduct a transient incompressible flow simulation through a T-junction using the `pimpleFoam` solver. Employ the k-epsilon RAS turbulence model to capture turbulent effects. The fluid exhibits non-Newtonian behavior characterized by the ArrheniusBirdCarreau transport model. In this configuration, the fluid viscosity simplifies to a constant Newtonian value (kinematic viscosity = 1.5e-05 m²/s), as the shear-rate dependence coefficients are set accordingly.

The inlet boundary employs a uniformTotalPressure condition to drive the flow, while the two outlets utilize fixed pressure conditions. Wall boundaries apply no-slip conditions for velocity, along with appropriate wall functions for turbulence variables (k, epsilon, nut) and temperature.

Convective fluxes for momentum (phi,U), turbulence scalars (k, epsilon), and temperature (T) utilize limitedLinear/LimitedLinearV schemes for stability. Pressure-velocity coupling uses the PIMPLE algorithm with 1 outer corrector and 2 pressure correctors. The GAMG solver with Gauss-Seidel smoothing is used for pressure with a tolerance of 1e-6 and initial relative tolerance of 0.01. Velocity, turbulence scalars, and temperature equations are solved using the smoothSolver with symGaussSeidel smoother, tolerance 1e-05, and initial relative tolerance of 0.1. No relaxation is applied to any equations.
